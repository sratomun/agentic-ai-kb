# Agent economies & marketplaces

*The emerging "agentic web" — agents that discover, negotiate, transact, and delegate with each other.*

## What it is
Multi-agent systems extended into economic primitives: marketplaces, auctions, negotiation, service-level agreements, and agent-to-agent commerce — built on the peer protocols ([[a2a-protocol]], [[acp-protocol]]) that let heterogeneous agents find and pay each other.

## Why it matters
The long-horizon structural bet: if agents transact autonomously, then **protocols, identity, and trust become economic infrastructure** — and whoever owns those layers owns the toll booths. Early and speculative, but a watch-node, because it's where [[agent-protocols]] and [[agent-security]] stop being plumbing and start being markets.

## What the evidence shows
**2025 foundations.** The substrate for studying agent societies arrived: **AgentSociety** is a large-scale social simulator integrating LLM-driven generative agents, a realistic societal environment, and a simulation engine for studying emergent interaction among agents and between agents and their environment (→ [[2025-02-12-arxiv-2502-08691v2-agentsociety-large-scale-simulation-of-llm-driven-generative]]) — the kind of testbed where market and coordination dynamics can be observed before they're deployed.
- _Honest note: this is the most speculative concept on the radar — a real census cluster (~207) but little settled deep work; treat as a directional watch-area._
