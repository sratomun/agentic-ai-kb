# Human-agent interaction & oversight

*How people stay in the loop — steering, supervising, correcting, and collaborating with agents.*

## What it is
The design dimension covering human-in-the-loop control, oversight interfaces, mixed-initiative work, and the cognitive effects of working alongside agents. The counterweight to autonomy: not "how capable is the agent" but "how well can a person direct and trust it."

## Why it matters
The practical answer to the autonomy/safety tension — agents that are *steerable and supervisable*, not just autonomous. This is how teams actually adopt agents without ceding control, and it's directly tied to the enterprise [[governance-gap]] (human-in-the-loop protocols are one of the missing governance pieces).

## What the evidence shows
**2025 foundations.** The most-cited result here is a caution about the *human* side of the loop: **"Your Brain on ChatGPT"** documents accumulation of **cognitive debt** — measurable reductions in engagement and retention — when people over-rely on an AI assistant for knowledge work (→ [[2025-06-10-arxiv-2506-08872v2-your-brain-on-chatgpt-accumulation-of-cognitive-debt]]). The implication for agent design: oversight isn't just a safety control, it's a guard against deskilling.
