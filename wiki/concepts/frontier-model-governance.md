# Frontier-model governance

*The mid-2026 shift toward state control over frontier AI models in the US — executive orders, draft legislation, and the first export controls on a model.*

## What it is
The policy layer above the agents: how governments regulate access to and deployment of the frontier models that back agentic systems. Distinct from the enterprise [[governance-gap]] (internal controls) — this is *external*, state-level governance. The research-side mirror is [[agent-security]] (same jailbreak threat class).

## Why it matters
**Model access is now a strategic and geopolitical variable, not just a procurement choice.** From Brazil, expect possible access fragmentation on top-tier models, and rising compliance baselines for anyone deploying frontier-model-backed agents. Whoever you build on can be constrained by policy you don't control.

## What the evidence shows
The June 2026 sequence of state moves:
- **EO "Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security" (Jun 2):** a secure-deployment framework, voluntary early government access (up to 30 days pre-release), an AI-cybersecurity clearinghouse — but *explicitly no licensing/preclearance* (→ [[2026-06-21-us-federal-ai-framework]]).
- **GAAIA discussion draft (Jun 4):** ~270 pages covering frontier governance, workforce, cybersecurity, and R&D, aimed at large foundation-model developers.
- **Anthropic export controls (Jun 12):** the **first export controls on a *model*** — Claude Fable 5 / Mythos 5 — blocking foreign-national access (→ [[2026-06-21-frontier-model-export-controls]]).
