# How To Become A Mechanistic Interpretability Researcher

**Blog** · Neel Nanda (Google DeepMind) · 2025-09-02 · [link](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/jP9KDyMkchuv6tHwm/how-to-become-a-mechanistic-interpretability-researcher)

**The take (attributed):** Nanda argues mech interp is "high-leverage, impactful, and learnable on your own with short feedback loops and modest compute" — and that the way in is to learn the minimum viable basics fast, then learn by *doing* research, because "mech interp is a fundamentally empirical science."

**Stake:** This is also a recruiting funnel — it's tied to applications for his MATS stream. Read it as both genuine field-building advice and talent-pipeline marketing for his team.

## Argument
His recommended path is three staged loops, organized by feedback speed:
- **Learn the ropes (≤1 month):** breadth over depth — code a transformer from scratch, learn the core mech-interp techniques and the field landscape, build linear-algebra intuitions (ARENA as the on-ramp). "Get your hands dirty: Do not just read things." Move on after a month even if you don't feel "done."
- **Learn with mini-projects:** 1–5 day projects to practice the *fast* skills (write/run/debug experiments).
- **Work up to full projects:** 1–2 week research sprints; continue the best ones; develop the *slow* skills (when to pivot) and *very slow* skills (generating good research ideas).

A notable thread: "Use LLMs extensively — they're not perfect, but are better at mech interp than you right now! They're a crucial learning tool (when used right!)." He frames research skill as a feedback-loop hierarchy (fast: hours; slow: weeks; very slow: months) and says don't try to learn all of it at once.

## Why it matters / where it cuts
Less a research thesis than a window into how a frontier safety lab thinks about *building the interpretability talent pipeline* — and a tell that even the field's leading practitioner now treats LLMs as a core research multiplier. Pairs with his "Highly Opinionated Advice on How to Write ML Papers" (May 2025) and his research-process sequence as Nanda's 2025 field-building output. So what: a clean external benchmark for "what good empirical-research onboarding looks like" — short loops, learn-by-doing, LLMs as the accelerant — transferable well beyond interp.
