# From Accuracy to Auditability: A Survey of Determinism in Financial AI Systems

**SURVEY** · arXiv [2605.23955v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23955v2) · 2026-05-11 · Ruizhe Zhou, Xiaoyang Liu, Gaoyuan Du, Yi Zheng et al.

**Why it matters:** Ties straight to your finance interest and to governed execution — determinism is the audit story regulators demand of financial agents.

## What it surveys
Reproducibility/determinism failures in regulated financial AI (credit risk, fraud, AML).

## Framework / taxonomy
A systems taxonomy of nondeterminism across three modalities — tabular models (explanation variance), graph networks (stochastic sampling, temporal asynchrony), and LLM agentic workflows (batch-dependent divergence, trajectory drift) — with a layered evaluation framework linking metrics (RBO, D_cos, TDI, PSD) to audit readiness.

## Notable points
- First-party experiments quantify explanation rank instability, GNN prediction-flip rates, and tensor-parallel output divergence.
- Frames determinism as an auditability requirement, not a nicety.
- Directly relevant to governed/audit-grade agent deployment.
