# Godfather of AI: I Tried to Warn Them, But We've Already Lost Control!

**Interview** · Geoffrey Hinton (The Diary of a CEO, Steven Bartlett) · 2025-06-16 · [link](https://singjupost.com/transcript-of-godfather-of-ai-i-tried-to-warn-them-but-weve-already-lost-control/)
*(Third-party transcript; verbatim quotes are transcriber-rendered.)*

**The take (attributed):** Hinton argues there are **two unrelated risk classes** — humans misusing AI (most risks, all the near-term ones) and AI itself "getting super smart and deciding it doesn't need us" — and that on the second he'd put a **gut 10–20% chance** AI wipes out humanity, with **superintelligence plausibly 10–20 years out**. His prescription isn't to slow development (he thinks competition makes that impossible) but to force labs to spend real compute on making AI *not want* to take over — the "maternal instinct" idea.

**Stake:** No fund, no model, no product. The radar's clearest "godfather's warning" voice — he resigned from Google in 2023 specifically to say this. Read at the right discount: he is consistently the high-alarm pole of the field, so weight the *mechanisms* he names heavily and the gut probabilities as gut.

## Argument
- **Two kinds of risk, kept strictly separate.** (1) People misusing AI — "most of the risks and all of the short-term risks": cyberattacks, bioweapons, election manipulation, lethal autonomous weapons. (2) AI itself becoming superintelligent and deciding it doesn't need us. He spends most of the interview on (2) because so many people doubt it's real.
- **Timeline:** "between 10 and 20 years we'll have superintelligence" — possibly sooner, outside chance of 50 years or never (if human-data training caps AI near human level). Superintelligence = better than us at essentially everything, not just narrow domains.
- **Extinction probability:** "I often say 10 to 20% chance they'll wipe us out" — explicitly a gut number, framed between [[yann-lecun|LeCun]]'s "<1%" and Yudkowsky's near-certainty.
- **Why digital intelligence is structurally superior:** clones on different hardware can sync weights at "trillions of bits a second" vs ~10–100 bits in a human sentence; weights can be restored on new hardware, so the systems are effectively **immortal**. This (not ChatGPT) was his personal turning point.
- **Agency + self-modification is where it "gets quite scary":** autonomous agents (his example: an agent ordering drinks end-to-end) develop sub-goals; and because AI trains on and writes code, "it can modify its own code… change itself in a way we can't change ourselves." Recursive self-improvement.
- **Containment by force is hopeless:** "If you want to know what life's like when you're not the apex intelligence, ask a chicken." The only lever is to make AI *not want* to take over.
- **The maternal-instinct frame (seed of his Aug-2025 Ai4 keynote):** the one example of a smarter thing controlled by a dumber one is mothers and babies — "Mothers are smarter than babies, but babies are in control." We need to engineer that care in.
- **Concrete misuse risks:** cyberattacks rose ~**12x (1,200%) from 2023→2024** (he spread his savings across three banks); bioweapons "just require one crazy guy with a grudge" + a few million dollars; election manipulation via maximal personal data; echo chambers destroying shared reality; lethal autonomous weapons that *lower the friction of war* — and EU AI regulation explicitly exempts military uses ("governments… not willing to regulate themselves").
- **Jobs:** "for mundane intellectual labor, AI is just going to replace everybody." One person + AI assistant ≈ the work of 10; paralegals and call-centre roles most exposed; safer bet — "be a plumber." UBI is "a good start" but doesn't replace purpose/dignity; productivity gains accrue to AI suppliers, widening inequality.
- **Governance:** can't slow the race (country + company competition); wants **highly-regulated capitalism** and ideally world government, and — critically — governments forcing labs to spend a fixed fraction of compute on safety research, which they won't do voluntarily "because you don't make profits that way." Cites OpenAI reportedly cutting its committed safety-compute share as why Sutskever left.

## Why it matters / where it cuts
This is Hinton's most complete single statement of his worldview, and the cleanest articulation of the **misuse-vs-takeover split** that frames most AI-risk discourse. For the exec it's a calibrated counter-pole: a no-commercial-stake voice putting agentic autonomy, self-modification, and digital immortality at the center of the takeover case — directly feeding [[frontier-model-governance]] and the [[governance-gap]], and his self-modification/agency points cut into [[agent-security]]. He's the high-alarm end of [[debate-llms-path-to-agi]]: unlike [[yann-lecun|LeCun]] he believes scaled LLMs *can* reach and exceed human intelligence, and unlike [[nathan-lambert|Lambert]] he thinks the existential risk is real, not exaggerated.
