# Get Good at Agents

**Blog** · Nathan Lambert (Interconnects) · 2026-01-21 · [link](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/get-good-at-agents)

**The take (attributed):** Lambert argues that applying yesterday's work habits to coding agents is "fundamentally wrong" — the uplift comes from working *more open-ended, more ambitious, more asynchronous*, and **agents "push the humans up the org chart."**

**Stake:** Practitioner-researcher; open-models advocate, low commercial stake — but talking up a workflow he's personally invested in.

## Argument
- Direct sequel to [[2026-01-09-blog-lambert-claude-code-hits-different]]. Lambert says his instinct to micromanage agents, set them on small tasks, and grind harder himself "would limit the uplift." The right direction is open-ended, ambitious, asynchronous work — "let the agents do most of the hard work."
- Per Lambert: "Every engineer needs to learn how to design systems. Every researcher needs to learn how to run a lab. **Agents push the humans up the org chart.**" His role shifts "more to pointing the army rather than using the power-tool."
- The new moat: "**Being good at using AI today is a better moat than working hard.**" / "Software is becoming free, good decision making in research, design, and product has never been so valuable."
- Concrete workflow he reports: GPT-5 Pro for planning, [[claude|Claude]] Code with Opus 4.5 for implementation, passing context back to GPT-5 Pro for deep search when stuck. Says Codex with [[gpt-5|GPT-5.2]] (xhigh) is "more meticulous than Claude even" but he hasn't cracked getting the best from it.
- Leaves Claude Code instances running on his DGX Spark implementing features in their RL codebase while away: "they make mistakes, they catch most of their own mistakes... but they're capable."
- The crucial open skill: "how do we stack these bricks into coherent long-term projects?" — moving agents from toy software to meaningful long-horizon tasks. No guides exist: "the only way is to play with them" — give an agent your *hardest* task and see what it gets stuck on.

## Why it matters / where it cuts
A grounded, first-person account of how a frontier practitioner is restructuring his own work around [[coding-agents]] — useful signal on the org-design and human-in-the-loop shift the exec tracks under [[human-agent-interaction]].
