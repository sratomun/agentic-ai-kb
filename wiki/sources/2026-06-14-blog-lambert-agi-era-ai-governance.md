# Welcome to the AGI era of AI governance

**Blog** · Nathan Lambert (Interconnects) · 2026-06-14 · [link](https://www.interconnects.ai/p/welcome-to-the-agi-era-of-ai-governance)
*(Note: post is partly paywalled; this captures the free portion.)*

**The take (attributed):** Lambert argues the US executive forcing [[anthropic|Anthropic]] to suspend access to its [[claude-fable-5|Claude 5 Fable]]/[[claude-mythos-5|Mythos]] models is "**the starting gun of a new era in AI governance**" — the AGI/agentic era — and that **export-banning model weights is a lasting, negative policy** while [[anthropic|Anthropic]]'s own fear-mongering "accelerated this moment."

**Stake:** Open-source advocate, low commercial stake — but pointedly critical of frontier-lab (esp. Anthropic) safety messaging, and warns his *own* open-source camp not to celebrate.

## Argument
- Frame: the saga marks the shift "from answer inference to agentic inference" (cites Stratechery's "The Inference Shift") — from the "ChatGPT era" of governance (models that answer questions) to the AGI era of agents that complement human workers.
- The events: government reached out to Anthropic to suspend access for foreign nationals / users abroad after a narrow Fable jailbreak; the White House was tipped off by Amazon (Anthropic's largest partner).
- His likely-lasting opinions: (1) an export ban on weights — open or closed — is "a lasting, negative policy for the U.S."; (2) the cybersecurity concern was legitimate but too narrow to justify this — "no model is perfectly immune to jailbreaks"; (3) "**Anthropic's constant fear-mongering over the last few years has accelerated this moment**" — comparing AI to nuclear weapons primed this governance style ("reaping what it has sowed"); (4) the open-source community shouldn't take a victory lap — it's economically destabilizing and could "pop the AI bubble"; (5) the White House action is heavy-handed and politically inclined against Anthropic; (6) unclear why Amazon went straight to the White House.
- Net stance: "I'm still on the side that the risks have been exaggerated, but the margin between the real risks... and the language used to describe them has shrunk."
- Sovereign AI: Europe, the Middle East, likely China realizing they could be left without frontier AI; warns open-source advocates "aren't remotely ready for when the spotlight... turns its gaze upon them" — an aggressive action against an open model is "very likely" (3 months to 2 years out).
