# Yann LeCun: a contrarian bet against LLMs (AMI Labs)

**Interview** · Yann LeCun (MIT Technology Review, Caiwei Chen) · 2026-01-22 · [link](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/)

**The take (attributed):** LeCun argues **LLMs are a dead end for human-level intelligence** — they can't reason, plan, or model the physical world — and the path forward is **[[world-models]] / JEPA**.

**Stake:** Founder/Executive Chairman of [[ami-labs]], building world models — directly talking his own (anti-LLM) book.

## Argument
- On LLMs: scaling them to human-level intelligence is "simply false." They are "limited to the discrete world of text… can't truly reason or plan, because they lack a model of the world… can't predict the consequences of their actions" (invokes the Moravec Paradox). Human-level AI "is not going to be built on LLMs"; "major conceptual breakthroughs have to happen."
- LLMs survive as "the orchestrator in systems" — "LLM plus a lot of things" (perception, code), not pure LLMs.
- Solution: **JEPA** (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture) — non-generative; learns an abstract representation of the world and predicts in that space, "ignoring the details you can't predict… like a baby learning about gravity." Trained on video/audio/sensor data, not just text.
- On agents: "An agentic system… cannot work reliably unless it has a world model to predict the consequences of its actions. Without it, the system will inevitably make mistakes." (Direct rebuttal to scaffold-the-LLM approaches.)
- On open vs closed: the closed frontier approach ([[openai]], [[anthropic]]) is "a strategic mistake"; "all leading open-source AI platforms are Chinese" ([[deepseek]]).
- On academia: "Don't work on LLMs. There is no point… breakthroughs are not going to come from scaling up LLMs."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The steel-manned **skeptic / paradigm-shift** position from a Turing laureate — the sharpest counter to the "agents = AGI now" thesis. Anchors the other side of the debate below; note the stake (he's raising/building the alternative).
