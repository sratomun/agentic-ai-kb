# DeepSeek-R1: Incentivizing Reasoning Capability in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning

**arXiv [2501.12948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948)** · 2025-01-22 · DeepSeek-AI

**Significance.** Shows reasoning can be incentivized in LLMs through pure RL without human-labeled reasoning trajectories, then packaged into a multi-stage pipeline (DeepSeek-R1) that matches OpenAI-o1-1217 on math, and distilled into small dense models that beat RL-on-small-models.

## Abstract
General reasoning represents a long-standing and formidable challenge in artificial intelligence. Recent breakthroughs, exemplified by large language models (LLMs) (Brown et al., 2020; OpenAI, 2023) and chain-of-thought prompting (Wei et al., 2022b), have achieved considerable success on foundational reasoning tasks. However, this success is heavily contingent upon extensive human-annotated demonstrations, and models' capabilities are still insufficient for more complex problems. Here we show that the reasoning abilities of LLMs can be incentivized through pure reinforcement learning (RL), obviating the need for human-labeled reasoning trajectories. The proposed RL framework facilitates the emergent development of advanced reasoning patterns, such as self-reflection, verification, and dynamic strategy adaptation. Consequently, the trained model achieves superior performance on verifiable tasks such as mathematics, coding competitions, and STEM fields, surpassing its counterparts trained via conventional supervised learning on human demonstrations. Moreover, the emergent reasoning patterns exhibited by these large-scale models can be systematically harnessed to guide and enhance the reasoning capabilities of smaller models.

## From the paper (full-text)
**Contribution / method.** Built on DeepSeek-V3-Base (671B MoE, 37B activated) using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO), with reward based solely on correctness of final predictions against ground truth (rule-based accuracy + format rewards), and no SFT phase before RL. This yields **DeepSeek-R1-Zero**, which develops reasoning behaviors (verification, reflection, alternative approaches, an "aha moment") purely through RL. Its limitations: poor readability and language mixing (mixing English and Chinese within a single chain-of-thought), plus weakness in non-reasoning areas like writing and open-domain QA. To fix this, **DeepSeek-R1** uses a multi-stage pipeline: (1) collect thousands of cold-start data with conversational, human-aligned thinking, then SFT; (2) reasoning-oriented RL to improve performance and language consistency; (3) rejection sampling + SFT again, incorporating both reasoning and non-reasoning data (~600k reasoning samples + ~200k non-reasoning samples = ~800k total); (4) a secondary RL stage for all scenarios to align with human preferences (helpfulness and harmlessness). Separately, R1's reasoning is **distilled** (SFT only, no RL) into open dense models — Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B, Qwen2.5-Math-7B, Qwen2.5-14B, Qwen2.5-32B, Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct — using the 800k samples generated by DeepSeek-R1.

**Results.** On the main benchmark table, **DeepSeek-R1** scores AIME 2024 (Pass@1) **79.8** vs OpenAI-o1-1217 **79.2**; MATH-500 (Pass@1) **97.3** vs o1-1217 **96.4**; Codeforces rating **2029** (96.3 percentile, outperforming 96.3% of human participants); GPQA Diamond **71.5**; LiveCodeBench (Pass@1-COT) **65.9**. **DeepSeek-R1-Zero** (pure RL, no SFT) lifted AIME 2024 pass@1 from an initial **15.6% to 77.9%**, rising to higher with self-consistency; its standalone scores include MATH-500 95.9, Codeforces rating 1444 (80.4 percentile), GPQA Diamond 75.8. **Distillation** results (Table 15): DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B scores AIME 2024 pass@1 **28.9**, MATH-500 **83.9** — surpassing GPT-4o-0513 (AIME 9.3, MATH 74.6) and Claude-3.5-Sonnet-1022 (AIME 16.0, MATH 78.3); Qwen-7B **55.5** / 92.8; Qwen-14B **69.7** / 93.9; Qwen-32B **72.6** / 94.3 (Codeforces rating 1691); Llama-8B **50.4** / 89.1; Llama-70B **70.0** / 94.5. **Distillation vs RL** (Table 16): DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-32B (AIME **72.6**, MATH 94.3, GPQA 62.1, LiveCodeBench 57.2) significantly beats Qwen2.5-32B-Zero — the same 32B base trained directly with large-scale RL for 10K+ steps (AIME 47.0, MATH 91.6) — across all benchmarks.

**Takeaway.** Distilling a powerful reasoning teacher into small dense models yields better small-model reasoning than running large-scale RL directly on those small models, which the authors note may not even reach distillation's performance despite enormous compute.
