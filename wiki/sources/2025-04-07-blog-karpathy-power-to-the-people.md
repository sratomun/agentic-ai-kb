# Power to the people: How LLMs flip the script on technology diffusion

**Blog** · Andrej Karpathy (karpathy.bearblog.dev) · 2025-04-07 · [link](https://karpathy.bearblog.dev/power-to-the-people/)

**The take (attributed):** Karpathy argues LLMs are the rare transformative technology that **diffuses bottom-up** — generating disproportionate benefit for individuals while their impact on corporations and governments is "muted and lagging" — the inverse of electricity, computers, the internet or GPS, which all flowed top-down from government/military → corporate → individual.

**Stake:** Educator, not vendor — no model or fund to talk up here. Read this as a genuine diffusion thesis, though note the optimism ("Power to the people... I love it") is characteristic of his communicator stance.

## Argument
- **The reversal:** ChatGPT is the fastest-growing consumer app in history (400M WAU at writing). Per Karpathy this is "a major multiplier to an individual's power level across a broad range of capabilities" with an "incredibly low" barrier — cheap/free, fast, on-demand, in any language.
- **Why orgs lag — capability shape:** LLMs are "versatile but also shallow and fallible" — quasi-expert across many domains. An organization's superpower is already *concentrating* deep expertise (engineers, lawyers, analysts), so the LLM only makes it "a bit better at the things it could already do." An individual is an expert in at most one thing, so the same broad quasi-expertise lets them do things they *couldn't do before* — "vibe code apps... approach legal documents... grok esoteric research papers... data analytics" without hiring an expert.
- **Why orgs lag — complexity & inertia:** orgs carry integrations, legacy systems, security, privacy, compliance, legal risk ("one disastrous hallucination away from losing your job") plus cultural/bureaucratic inertia. "You can't just vibe code something."
- **The forward risk — dynamic range:** today frontier performance is cheap and flat ("Money can't buy better ChatGPT. Bill Gates talks to GPT 4o just like you do"). Train-time scaling, test-time scaling and ensembles *increase* the dynamic range; [[distillation]] *decreases* it. The moment money *can* buy dramatically better intelligence, large orgs and elites re-concentrate the advantage — "Their child will be tutored by GPT-8-pro-max-high, yours by GPT-6 mini."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
This is the sharpest articulation of the **[[agent-adoption]] asymmetry** the exec lives inside: the consumer/individual unlock is already here, but enterprise value is gated by exactly the complexity-and-control problems the radar's [[governance-gap]] and [[agent-security]] nodes track. The decision-relevant warning is the dynamic-range point — the current "shockingly distributed" equality is contingent on frontier performance staying cheap and flat; if [[distillation]] loses to test-time scaling, advantage re-concentrates toward whoever can spend. The [[vibe-coding]] examples here predate his later vibe-coding-vs-agentic-engineering split and show the empowerment side of that term.
