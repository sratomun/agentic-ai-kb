# Three Years from GPT-3 to Gemini 3

**Blog** · Ethan Mollick (One Useful Thing) · 2025-11-18 · [link](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/three-years-from-gpt-3-to-gemini)

**The take (attributed):** Mollick argues the Gemini 3 / Antigravity moment marks the shift "from chatbots to agents" — and that coding agents are really **general-purpose tools**, because "everything you do on a computer is, ultimately, code." "The era of the chatbot is turning into the era of the digital coworker."

**Stake:** Wharton/UPenn professor; Co-Intelligence author; hands-on adoption-optimist with early Google access. Weight the "coding agents = general-purpose tools" thesis and the human-in-the-loop reframing; discount the optimism.

## Argument
- Demonstrates capability gain by prompt rather than benchmark: gave Gemini 3 his Nov-2022 first post and "show how far AI has come… by doing stuff" — it built a playable starship simulator. "In 2022, AI could describe the engine. In 2025, AI can code the engine, design the interface, and let you pilot the ship."
- **Coding tools that aren't just coding tools:** Google's **Antigravity** (akin to [[coding-agents|Claude Code / Codex]]). His central move: don't dismiss these as programmer tools — "the ability to code isn't just about programming, it's about being able to do anything that happens on a computer." That makes coding agents general-purpose. Antigravity's **Inbox** lets you dispatch agents that ping you for permission; he runs four at once, communicating "in English" while they "use code to do the work."
- Demo: pointed Antigravity at his newsletter directory, asked for a site of his past AI predictions plus a web check on which were right/wrong. It read files, planned (he edited/approved), did web research, built the site, took over his browser to verify. His honest caveat: "agents aren't there yet" — errors were "individual judgement calls or human-like misunderstandings," not classic AI failures.
- **"PhD-level intelligence?"** Gave Gemini 3 a decade-old, messy crowdfunding research directory and asked it to clean the data and write an original journal-style paper. It generated hypotheses, ran statistics, even invented its own NLP-based novelty measure, and produced a 14-page paper — with a grad student's weaknesses (some methods/theorizing overreached). "We have moved past hallucinations… to more subtle, and often human-like, concerns."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The clearest dated statement of his "chatbots → agents/digital coworker" thesis, and his strongest articulation that **coding harnesses are general knowledge-work tools** — directly relevant to [[agent-adoption]] and [[coding-agents]]. His reframe — "'human in the loop' is evolving from 'human who fixes AI mistakes' to 'human who directs AI work'" — is the bridge to his management thesis in [[2026-01-27-blog-mollick-management-as-ai-superpower|Management as AI superpower]].
