# State of FinOps 2026 — AI becomes default scope

**Why it matters:** The hard data behind [[agent-finops]] — AI spend went from a fringe concern to near-universal FinOps scope in two years, and cost management moved up the org chart.

## What it found
- **98% of FinOps practitioners now manage AI spend**, up from 63% in 2025 and 31% in 2024 (n≈1,192). 90% now manage SaaS (up from 65%), 64% licensing, 57% private cloud.
- **AI-cost management is the single most-wanted skillset (58% of respondents).**
- The Foundation formally changed its mission from "managing the Value of Cloud" to "managing the Value of **Technology**" — FinOps has outgrown cloud-only.
- FinOps is rising organizationally: per CIO Dive's reporting on the data, **78% of teams report to the CTO/CIO (up from 61% in 2023)**; CFO-aligned dropped to ~8%, and CTO/CIO-aligned teams report 2–4× more influence over technology selection. Context: Gartner projects global IT spend +9.8% YoY past $6T in 2026.

## So what
Cost governance for AI/agents is now a default enterprise function with a skills shortage and exec-level reporting lines — the demand-side case for treating budget as a control plane, not a dashboard. Discount appropriately: these are self-reported figures from a self-selected membership sample; the CIO Dive write-up is the independent relay rather than fresh primary data.
