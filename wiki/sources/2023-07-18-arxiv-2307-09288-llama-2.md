# Llama 2: Open Foundation and Fine-Tuned Chat Models

**arXiv [2307.09288](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.09288)** · 2023-07-18 · Hugo Touvron, Louis Martin, Kevin Stone et al. (GenAI, Meta)

**Significance.** Meta's open-weight chat models and the most detailed published RLHF post-training recipe of its era. Its distinctive moves — **two separate reward models** (helpfulness and safety), **rejection sampling combined with PPO**, and **Ghost Attention** for multi-turn instruction adherence — became reference points for open post-training.

## Abstract
In this work, we develop and release Llama 2, a collection of pretrained and fine-tuned large language models (LLMs) ranging in scale from 7 billion to 70 billion parameters. Our fine-tuned LLMs, called Llama 2-Chat, are optimized for dialogue use cases. Our models outperform open-source chat models on most benchmarks we tested, and based on our human evaluations for helpfulness and safety, may be a suitable substitute for closed-source models. We provide a detailed description of our approach to fine-tuning and safety improvements of Llama 2-Chat in order to enable the community to build on our work and contribute to the responsible development of LLMs.

## From the paper (full-text)
**Contribution / method.** Pipeline is Pretraining → SFT → iterative RLHF. **SFT** — "quality is all you need": they stopped after **27,540** high-quality vendor annotations, training 2 epochs (cosine schedule, initial LR 2e-5, batch size 64, seq len 4096) with loss zeroed on prompt tokens. **Preference data** — binary comparisons where annotators also rate the margin (significantly / better / slightly / negligibly better); **over 1M Meta comparisons** (1,418,091), combined with open-source sets for ~2.9M total, collected weekly so reward models stay on-distribution. **Reward modeling** — because helpfulness and safety trade off, they train **two separate RMs (Helpfulness RM and Safety RM)**, each initialized from a chat checkpoint, using the InstructGPT-style binary ranking loss **plus a margin term** keyed to the preference rating. **RLHF (V1–V5)** uses two algorithms: **Rejection Sampling** (sample K outputs, keep the best by the RM, fine-tune on it — done only on the 70B model and distilled into smaller ones) and **PPO** (with KL penalty); they used rejection sampling alone through V4, then applied PPO on top of the rejection-sampling checkpoint. **Ghost Attention (GAtt)** concatenates a persistent system instruction across turns during SFT (loss zeroed on prior turns) so the model keeps following it over many turns.

**Results.** Pretrained Llama 2 70B beats Llama 1 65B by ≈5 points on MMLU and ≈8 on BBH, and outperforms all open-source base models tested; it is competitive with GPT-3.5 on MMLU (68.9 vs 70.0) and GSM8K (56.8 vs 57.1) but trails on coding (HumanEval 29.9). For chat: **Llama 2-Chat 70B has a 36% win rate and 31.5% tie rate vs ChatGPT** in human eval; Llama 2-Chat 34B exceeds a 75% win rate against Vicuna-33B and Falcon 40B. Safety preference data binned into safe/safe and unsafe responses at **18% / 47% / 35%**. Reward-model accuracy (Table 7): Helpfulness RM 70.6 avg, Safety RM 64.3 avg, both beating GPT-4 as a preference judge on the Meta test sets. With GAtt, Llama 2-Chat keeps referring to a defined attribute **100% of the time for up to 20+ turns**.

**Takeaway.** Splitting the reward signal into helpfulness and safety RMs, and pairing rejection sampling with PPO across iterative RLHF rounds, yields open chat models competitive with ChatGPT — with GAtt as a cheap fix for multi-turn instruction drift.
