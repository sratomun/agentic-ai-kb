# AI research is a max-performance domain

**Blog** · Jason Wei (jasonwei.net) · 2025-05-29 · [link](https://www.jasonwei.net/blog/ai-research-is-a-max-performance-domain)

**The take (attributed):** Wei argues frontier AI research is a **"max-performance domain"** — you can be world-class by being elite at one thing (training the best model, starting a paradigm, building an adopted benchmark) while being incompetent at everything adjacent; "the metric is the best five works in your career, not the average."

**Stake:** OpenAI/Meta researcher describing the talent economics of the labs he works in — a culture/management view, not a technical claim. Read as an insider's account of how frontier research rewards talent (and why the labs hire and tolerate the way they do).

## Argument
- A max-performance domain rewards seminal impact and forgives incompetence elsewhere — Wei has seen "game-changing AI researchers" with terrible presentation skills, no political awareness, no career planning; "a top AI researcher who probably wouldn't pass a basic coding interview."
- Consistency is irrelevant: "tens of failed projects per year" is fine if you produce a seminal work every few years.
- **Danger:** over-indexing on role models — you can't tell whether you're copying the good traits; a star can make a bad political move or a bold unsubstantiated claim that only works "because of who they are."
- Exponential upside, little downside → "interviews are especially useless in domains like AI research, because they tend to severely punish mistakes and don't capture exponential value."
- Contrast: non-max-performance domains demand strengths *and* no weaknesses (a soccer defender, a concert pianist).

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The clearest insider statement of **why frontier labs hire, retain, and bet the way they do** — spiky-genius talent over well-rounded competence, portfolios judged by their top-5 outcomes. For the exec, decision-relevant as a lens on AI-lab org design and talent strategy (and a caution that "interview-style" filtering misses exactly the exponential-value people). Connects to [[jason-wei]]'s broader thesis that as AI handles the routine, more work becomes max-performance.
