# The Five Ws of Multi-Agent Communication: Who Talks to Whom, When, What, and Why -- A Survey from MARL to Emergent Language and LLMs

**SURVEY** · arXiv [2602.11583v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11583v1) · 2026-02-12 · Jingdi Chen, Hanqing Yang, Zongjun Liu, Carlee Joe-Wong

**Why it matters:** The clearest framework for thinking about *how* agents should talk to each other — complements the protocol-standards view with the learning view.

## What it surveys
Multi-agent communication across three paradigms — MARL, Emergent Language, and LLM-based systems.

## Framework / taxonomy
Organizes the field by the Five Ws (who talks to whom, what, when, why), tracing evolution from hand-designed/learned MARL protocols → emergent symbolic language → LLM natural-language collaboration.

## Notable points
- Names the core trade-offs: task-specificity and interpretability of learned protocols vs grounding/generalization/scalability of emergent language.
- Distills practical design patterns for hybrid learning+language+control systems.
- Useful bridge between the RL-MAS and LLM-MAS literatures that rarely cite each other.
