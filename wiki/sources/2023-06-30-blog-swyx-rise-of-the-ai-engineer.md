# The Rise of the AI Engineer

**Blog** · swyx (Shawn Wang) · 2023-06-30 · [link](https://www.latent.space/p/ai-engineer)

**The take (attributed):** swyx argues a **new engineering discipline — the "AI Engineer"** — is emerging to *wield* foundation models via API rather than train them, and that it "will likely be the highest-demand engineering job of the decade."

**Stake:** swyx literally named the role and went on to build the AI Engineer conference + media empire (ai.engineer, Latent Space) around it — read the prediction knowing he then made it his business to make it true.

## Argument
- A "shift right" of applied AI: tasks that took "5 years and a research team in 2013" now take "API docs and a spare afternoon." Karpathy is cited: there will be far more AI Engineers than ML engineers, and "one can be quite successful in this role without ever training anything."
- The role emerges because the supply math forces it: "~5000 LLM researchers in the world, but ~50m software engineers" — labs sell "AI Research as a Service" APIs, and an "in-between class" arises to use them.
- "When it comes to shipping AI products, you want engineers, not researchers." He predicts the "flippening": AI Engineer jobs will overtake ML Engineer jobs within 5 years.
- Frames the architectural divide that recurs in his later work: **"software atop intelligence" vs "intelligent software"** (Code Core vs LLM Core) — re-establishing human-written code to orchestrate LLM power (LangChain, Voyager). Names [[tool-use]] tooling (LangChain, LlamaIndex) and early autonomous agents (Auto-GPT, BabyAGI) as the AI Engineer's stack.
