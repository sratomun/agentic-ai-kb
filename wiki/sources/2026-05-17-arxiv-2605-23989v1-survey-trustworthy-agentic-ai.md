# Towards trustworthy agentic AI: a comprehensive survey of safety, robustness, privacy, and system security

**SURVEY** · arXiv [2605.23989v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23989v1) · 2026-05-17 · Jinhu Qi, Muzhi Li, Jiahong Liu, Yuqin Shu et al.

**Why it matters:** The most deployment-oriented security survey of 2026 — useful as the reference frame when you need to argue what 'safe enough to ship' means for an agent.

## What it surveys
Trustworthy agentic AI for high-risk deployments, organized around two dimensions: Safety & Robustness, and Privacy & System Security.

## Framework / taxonomy
Maps where risks emerge along the agent workflow and gives stage-targeted mitigations; consolidates evaluation into a unified metrics-and-benchmarks hub emphasizing process signals (constraint violations, trace completeness, adversarial success rates) with scenario-to-metric guidance for release gating.

## Notable points
- Treats trustworthiness as a deployment-gating problem, not a research aside — explicit release-gating guidance.
- Open challenges: self-evolving agents, runtime monitoring/verification, privacy-preserving personalization, the trust-utility trade-off.
- Includes a case study of real-world security failures in open-source agentic systems.
