# 2026: This is AGI

**Blog** · Sonya Huang & Pat Grady (Sequoia Capital) · 2026-01-14 · [link](https://sequoiacap.com/article/2026-this-is-agi/)

**The take (attributed):** Huang & Grady argue that **long-horizon agents are *functionally* AGI and have already arrived** — 2026 is their year.

**Stake:** Sequoia Partners; this is an investment thesis essay, bullish on the agent application layer the firm funds.

## Argument
- They offer a deliberately *functional* (not technical) definition: "AGI is the ability to figure things out." An AI that can figure things out now has the three human ingredients — **knowledge** (pre-training, the 2022 ChatGPT moment), **reasoning** (inference-time compute, o1 in late 2024), and **iteration** (long-horizon agents, "in the last few weeks" as coding agents crossed a threshold).
- Two approaches are both scaling: **RL** (research labs, keeping models on-task longer) and **agent harnesses** (the application layer — scaffolding around model limits like memory hand-offs/compaction; they name Manus, Claude Code, Factory Droids).
- Their headline data point: **METR's long-task curve doubling ~every 7 months**, extrapolated to a human-day of work by 2028, a year by 2034, a century by 2037.
- "So what": you can soon *hire* an agent — they list domain agents functioning as specialist/associate/pen-tester/SRE/recruiter/etc.

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The cleanest articulation of the **agent-bull case** from a top-tier VC — useful as the steel-manned optimist position. Read with the stake in mind, and note their own caveat ("agents still fail… hallucinate, lose context, charge confidently down the wrong path"). Anchors one side of the debate below.
