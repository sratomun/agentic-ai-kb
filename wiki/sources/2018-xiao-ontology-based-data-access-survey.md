# Ontology-Based Data Access: A Survey

**IJCAI 2018** · Xiao, Calvanese, Kontchakov, Lembo, Poggi, Rosati, Zakharyaschev · [pdf](https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2018/0777.pdf)

**Significance.** The standard modern survey of OBDA — the reference for [[ontology-based-data-access]] principles, the rewriting pipeline, and systems.

## Contribution (foundational)
Consolidates a decade of OBDA into one reference: the architecture (ontology + mappings + sources), the **query-answering-by-rewriting** pipeline (ontology query → rewriting w.r.t. the TBox → unfolding through mappings → SQL over sources), the role of DL-Lite for tractability, mapping languages (R2RML), optimizations (e.g., handling redundancy, semantic indexing), and mature systems (Mastro, **Ontop**). It surveys extensions and open problems (mapping management, evolution, beyond-relational sources). The canonical entry point for anyone building the alignment/mapping tier of a mediated semantic architecture.
