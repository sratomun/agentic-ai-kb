# Hassabis: Preparing for AI's Endgame (TIME100 2025)

**Interview / profile** · Demis Hassabis (TIME, Andrew R. Chow) · 2025-04-16 · [link](https://time.com/7277608/demis-hassabis-interview-time100-2025/)

**The take (attributed):** Hassabis argues AGI is **5–10 years away** — deliberately more conservative than [[openai|Altman]] or [[anthropic|Amodei]] — and frames it through a **scientist-first bar**: not "economically valuable tasks" but a system that could *invent* new explanations of the universe (re-derive general relativity from Einstein's information; pose a new mathematical conjecture, not just solve one). He pairs radical optimism (disease, energy, "radical abundance") with unusually candid warnings on misalignment, dual-use bio risk, and the geopolitics of an AGI race.

**Stake:** CEO of [[google-deepmind|Google DeepMind]] (the lab building [[gemini|Gemini]]) and a 2024 Nobel laureate. He's talking his own book on two fronts: that the science-via-AI thesis is the right one (it's his lab's flagship), and that Google's **closed-weights, gradual-release** safety posture is the responsible one (contra [[deepseek|DeepSeek]]/[[meta-ai|Meta]] open weights). Read the abundance vision and the "we're the safe ones" framing at a discount; weight his capability reads heavily — it's his domain and his data.

## Argument
- **AGI definition is contested, and his bar is higher.** Where [[openai|OpenAI]] defines AGI as out-performing humans on most economically valuable tasks, Hassabis wants a system that can produce genuinely new science — "come up with general relativity with only the information Einstein had," or theorize (not just solve) a new math hypothesis. "I identify myself as a scientist first and foremost."
- **Timeline: 5–10 years** — "rather pessimistic… by industry standards." An AI agent that can *meaningfully automate AI research itself* is, he predicts, "a few years away."
- **AlphaFold is the template, but narrow.** It won a Nobel yet "has no understanding of the wider world, cannot carry out research." The dream is to generalize that scientific-discovery capability — the through-line to his abundance vision (all disease solved, zero-carbon free energy, resource conflicts dissolved).
- **Project Astra = scaffolding, not the point.** The universal Gemini assistant is, he says, building "the necessary scaffolding" for AI that will one day make its own discoveries — and agents will carry out "increasingly more complex tasks independently."
- **Two risk buckets.** (1) Capability uplift for bad actors (e.g. bioweapon synthesis) → hence careful capability-testing, gradual release, and **keeping the weights of the most powerful models out of public hands** so they can be withdrawn. (2) Autonomous-system risk — misalignment, "strategic lying" already observed; staying "in charge of… self-improving systems" is "an extremely difficult challenge."
- **The real bottleneck is political coordination, not the tech.** Even safe labs don't stop *unsafe* AIs proliferating; that needs international cooperation just as US–China realpolitik and AI militarization are eroding it. Chip export controls are "fine" but he'd avoid an "antagonistic" posture.
- **The military red-line reversal (the uncomfortable part).** TIME notes the 2014 contractual ban on military use of DeepMind tech has "quietly disappeared" through restructures; Gemini-lineage systems are now sold via Google to militaries. Hassabis frames it as pragmatism — the world became "a much more dangerous place," "we can't take for granted… that democratic values are going to win out." TIME's pointed read: if the red line didn't hold when AGI was distant, what holds when it's close?
- **Labor + politics.** He concedes he forecasts tech better than economics, wishes more economists took near-term AGI seriously, and says we'll need "a new political philosophy" — democracy "is not a panacea" and may give way to "something better."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
This is the canonical statement of the **Hassabis stance** the radar should hold him to: AGI is near (5–10y from early 2025), the current paradigm is mostly right but missing pieces, and the destination is *scientific superintelligence*, not just task automation. It anchors the "capabilities are nearly there / scaffold-the-LLM-plus-RL works" pole of [[debate-llms-path-to-agi]] — the steel-manned opposite of [[yann-lecun|LeCun]]'s "LLMs are a dead end." Note where it cuts the other way: his own admissions (misalignment unsolved, the military reversal) are the strongest material for discounting the abundance narrative.
