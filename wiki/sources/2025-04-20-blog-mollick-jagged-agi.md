# On Jagged AGI: o3, Gemini 2.5, and everything after

**Blog** · Ethan Mollick (One Useful Thing) · 2025-04-20 · [link](https://www.oneusefulthing.org/p/on-jagged-agi-o3-gemini-25-and-everything)

**The take (attributed):** Mollick argues o3 and Gemini 2.5 are best understood as **"Jagged AGI"** — superhuman in enough areas to change how we work, yet unreliable enough that human expertise is still needed to know where they break. And crucially, their **agentic** properties may speed AI's diffusion far faster than "normal technology" precedent suggests.

**Stake:** Wharton/UPenn professor; Co-Intelligence author; coined the "Jagged Frontier." Adoption-optimist running hands-on demos with brand-new models. Weight the agentic-diffusion hedge and the jaggedness frame; discount the "feeling the AGI" register.

## Argument
- Sets up the measurement problem (human tests don't fit AI; AI scores swing on phrasing per his Wharton paper; AI now passes the Turing Test, which we can no longer interpret) — so AGI claims are murky. Responds to **Tyler Cowen** calling o3 AGI.
- Demonstrates **agentic** behavior: a single vague cheese-shop prompt → o3 ranked slogans, did web research, made a logo, built financial/marketing plans, and launched a demo site in under 2 minutes; plus a "geo-guesser" photo demo and a one-prompt spreadsheet→statistical-PDF. Stresses o3 is a [[reasoning-models|Reasoner]] that uses tools and plans multi-step ([[tool-use]]).
- Re-ups his **Jagged Frontier**: AI beats experts on hard problems yet fails the modified surgeon brainteaser (pattern-matching the classic riddle from training). Verdict: "Jagged AGI."
- **Diffusion hedge:** even if it's AGI, social/org structures change slowly (AI "as a normal technology"). *But* agentic capability — decompose goals, use tools, navigate human systems "on its own, rather than requiring integration" — "might actually accelerate diffusion dramatically… we might hit adoption thresholds much faster than historical precedent would suggest." Whether there are hard capability thresholds is an open question — "the honest answer is we don't know."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The earliest 2025 source in his catalog and the conceptual root of his later adoption work: the **Jagged Frontier** is the lens behind the [[2026-01-27-blog-mollick-management-as-ai-superpower|delegation equation]], and the agentic-diffusion hedge is his first articulation that **agency could collapse the usual tech-adoption lag** — directly relevant to [[agent-adoption]]. Useful counterweight to "AI as normal technology" arguments.
