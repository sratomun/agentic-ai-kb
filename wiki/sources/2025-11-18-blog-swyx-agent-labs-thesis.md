# The Agent Labs Thesis (Welcome to GPT Wrapper Summer)

**Blog** · swyx (Shawn Wang) · 2025-11-18 · [link](https://www.latent.space/p/agent-labs)

**The take (attributed):** swyx argues a new company class — the **"Agent Lab"** — is winning by *researching and selling agents* rather than frontier models, and that the frontier labs ([[openai|OpenAI]], [[anthropic|Anthropic]]) are now blessing this application layer by pivoting to be "AI Clouds" for third-party builders.

**Stake:** He coins the term at his own conference; the thesis flatters the application-layer builders who fill his audience, talks, and sponsor list. Read the "open season for Agent Labs" optimism at that discount.

## Argument
- Rejects Steph Palazzolo's "Neolab" ("just being 'new' isn't a business plan"). **Agent Labs** = Cursor ($29B), Perplexity ($20B), [[devin|Cognition]] ($10B), Sierra ($10B), plus AI-transitioned incumbents (Notion, Vercel, Glean, Replit) and "Agent Labs within Model Labs" (Claude Code $1B ARR, Codex).
- **Model Labs vs Agent Labs** (driven by Conway's Law — priorities show in resource allocation):
  - *Product first, model last* (Cursor forked VSCode, understood users 2 years, then trained).
  - *Outcome-based pricing*: Agent Labs can charge $2000/mo or per outcome — "you measurably replace some subsection of human labor" → higher margins ([[agent-economies]]).
  - *Autonomy*: Model Labs favor lightweight harnesses (Building Effective Agents, Skills) betting the next model upgrade undoes harness gains; Agent Labs prize speed + human-in-the-loop control and "don't mind rewriting the harness every few months."
- **Open season** evidence: Epoch estimates all OpenAI inference is only ~28% of its compute (majority is unpublished research); the OpenAI Foundation livestream where Sam said "AI Cloud" and quoted the Bill Gates Line — "Sam has never spoken so clearly about OpenAI's intention to serve third-party builders at the app layer."
- **Why now**: model diversity ("Agents are Systems. The Model Selector is a Lie" — agents are bundles of model+prompt+memory+tools+planning+orchestration+auth); and the **pretraining → RL shift** ([[agentic-rl]]) — pretraining near its data limits, "the RL age rewards domain focus/dedicated environments," letting Agent Labs (Cursor, Cognition) start from open weights and do continued training to close the gap with frontier models.
- Bear case: GPT5's evaded omnimodality and Fidji Simo's "Moving beyond one-size-fits-all" hint the unified-AGI "G" vision may be fracturing into task-specific models.

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The sharpest current articulation of *where AI value accrues* — application/agent layer vs model layer — directly decision-relevant for anyone weighing build-vs-buy or where to place a career/bet. It is the macro-strategy capstone of swyx's "applied beats research at the product layer" thread that started in [[2023-06-30-blog-swyx-rise-of-the-ai-engineer|Rise of the AI Engineer]], and leans on the same [[agentic-rl|RL-over-pretraining]] turn the radar tracks in research.
