# 2025: The year in LLMs

**Blog** · Simon Willison (simonwillison.net) · 2025-12-31 · [link](https://simonwillison.net/2025/Dec/31/the-year-in-llms/)

**The take (attributed):** Willison's annual synthesis declares 2025 "the year of agents" — but on his deflationary definition (an LLM that runs tools in a loop), and his headline categories are **coding** and **search**, not the "magic assistant" he'd predicted wouldn't arrive.

**Stake:** Independent — third in his annual end-of-year series (after AI-in-2023, LLMs-in-2024); a survey of the whole field, not a product.

## Argument
The agent-relevant threads, in the order he names them:
- **The year of agents.** He'd started 2025 predicting agents "were not going to happen" — and grades himself "half right": "the science fiction version of a magic computer assistant that does anything you ask of (Her) didn't materialize." But on the working definition he adopted by September — "an LLM that runs tools in a loop to achieve a goal" / "LLM systems that can perform useful work via tool calls over multiple steps" — "agents are here and they are proving to be extraordinarily useful." "The two breakout categories for agents have been for coding and for search."
- **The year of coding agents and Claude Code.** "Claude Code is the most prominent example of what I call coding agents—LLM systems that can write code, execute that code, inspect the results and then iterate further."
- **The year of YOLO and the Normalization of Deviance.** Running agents with auto-confirmation (YOLO mode) "feels like a completely different product" — but he frames the casual acceptance of its risks as a *normalization of deviance*.
- Other named threads include: LLMs on the command-line, top-ranked Chinese open-weight models, "long tasks," **the year of the lethal trifecta**, "the year of the snitch," vibe coding, "alarmingly AI-enabled browsers," conformance suites, and "the year of slop."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
This is his canonical year-end map of where agents actually landed in 2025 — useful as a ground-truth checkpoint against vendor narratives. The two load-bearing claims for the radar: agents are real but *narrow* (coding + search are the breakouts, not general assistants), and the security tax (lethal trifecta, YOLO normalization-of-deviance) is now a named, recurring feature of the landscape rather than a fringe worry.
