# AI tokens: How to navigate AI's new spend dynamics

**Why it matters:** The best analyst-grade framing of *why* AI spend breaks the old cloud-TCO model — and the outside-in counterpart to the [[2026-05-09-arxiv-2605-09104v1-token-economics-llm-agents|Token Economics]] survey within [[agent-finops]].

## What it argues
AI must be governed as an economic system priced in **tokens** — the new unit of value — which makes spend "inherently variable and often unpredictable." Concrete points:
- AI is the **fastest-growing line in tech budgets, consuming up to half of IT spend at some firms**; enterprise cloud bills rose **~19% in 2025**.
- ROI lags: nearly half of leaders expect up to 3 years to see ROI from basic AI automation; only **28% of global finance leaders report clear measurable value from AI**.
- A Deloitte simulation finds an on-prem "AI factory" can deliver **>50% cost savings over 3 years vs API/neocloud** above a token threshold, with **~50% of AI-factory cost coming from non-GPU factors** (power, cooling, networking, facilities, software).
- Prescription: right-size models, cap context windows, real-time monitoring, budget alerts/chargebacks, and a formal FinOps practice.

## So what
The token is the unit to budget, attribute, and forecast against — and the cost stack is far more than GPUs. Useful exec framing for build-vs-buy (on-prem AI factory economics) and for why agent workloads need in-loop cost controls. Caveat: a consultancy talking its book on AI strategy services, though the figures are attributed.
