# Tülu 3: Pushing Frontiers in Open Language Model Post-Training

**arXiv [2411.15124](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.15124)** · 2024-11-22 · Nathan Lambert, Jacob Morrison, Valentina Pyatkin, Shengyi Huang, Hamish Ivison et al. (Allen Institute for AI / University of Washington)

**Significance.** The first fully-open, end-to-end modern post-training recipe — data, code, weights, and eval suite all released — built on Llama 3.1 base models. It closes the open-vs-closed gap (8B/70B beat their Llama 3.1 / Qwen 2.5 instruct counterparts and rival GPT-4o-mini and Claude 3.5 Haiku) and introduces RLVR, reinforcement learning against a verifier instead of a learned reward model.

## Abstract
Language model post-training is applied to refine behaviors and unlock new skills across a wide range of language models, but open recipes for applying these techniques lag behind proprietary ones. The underlying training data and recipes for post-training are simultaneously the most important pieces of the puzzle and the portion with the least transparency. To bridge this gap, we introduce Tülu 3, a family of fully-open state-of-the-art post-trained models, alongside its data, code, and training recipes, serving as a comprehensive guide for modern post-training techniques. Tülu 3, which builds on Llama 3.1 base models, achieves results surpassing the instruct versions of Llama 3.1, Qwen 2.5, Mistral, and even closed models such as GPT-4o-mini and Claude 3.5-Haiku. The training algorithms for our models include supervised finetuning (SFT), Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), and a novel method we call Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR). With Tülu 3, we build a multi-task evaluation scheme for post-training with development and unseen evaluations, standard benchmark implementations, and substantial decontamination of existing open datasets on said benchmarks. We conclude with analysis and discussion of training methods that did not reliably improve performance.

## From the paper (full-text)
**Contribution / method.** A four-stage open recipe over Llama 3.1 base (8B / 70B / 405B). **(1) Data curation** — survey and decontaminate public datasets (WildChat, Open Assistant, FLAN v2, a decontaminated UltraFeedback subset, OpenMathInstruct, Evol-CodeAlpaca, etc.), plus **persona-driven synthetic data** generated with GPT-4o using ~250K personas from Persona Hub to target math, code, and precise instruction following. Prompts are decontaminated against the eval suite with 8-gram matching at a 50% token-overlap threshold. **(2) SFT** — train 2 epochs, effective batch size 128, max length 4,096; they flag a "sum loss" (not averaging) over tokens as important for correct batch aggregation. **(3) Preference tuning (DPO)** — build an on-policy preference set by generating 4 completions per prompt from a model pool (incl. Tülu 3-SFT) and labeling with GPT-4o as judge across helpfulness/instruction-following/honesty/truthfulness; **354,192** preference instances; they use **length-normalized DPO** rather than PPO for simplicity/cost. **(4) RLVR** — a new RL stage that gives a **constant reward (α = 10) only when a completion is verifiably correct** (else 0), optimized with PPO over GSM8K, MATH, and IFEval prompts; the value model is initialized from a general reward model, and an asynchronous Ray + vLLM setup scales it to 70B/405B.

**Results.** (Development eval suite, Table 2 / Table 6.) **Tülu 3 8B averages 65.1**, ahead of Llama 3.1 8B Instruct (62.9) and Qwen 2.5 7B Instruct (66.5 avg but lower on several skills); **Tülu 3 70B averages 76.2**, surpassing GPT-3.5 Turbo (64.7), GPT-4o-mini (69.6), and Claude 3.5 Haiku (75.3). Stage progression at 8B (SFT → DPO → final RLVR): **GSM8K 76.2 → 84.3 → 87.6**, **MATH 31.5 → 42.0 → 43.7**, **IFEval (prompt loose) 72.8 → 81.1 → 82.4** — each stage adds on the targeted skills. RLVR specifically lifts GSM8K, MATH, and IFEval. Reported unfruitful methods: online DPO and rejection sampling did not give gains worth their compute.

**Takeaway.** A reproducible open recipe — SFT, then length-normalized DPO, then RLVR against a verifier — plus aggressive decontamination and persona-driven synthetic data is enough to match or beat closed instruct models at 8B and 70B.
