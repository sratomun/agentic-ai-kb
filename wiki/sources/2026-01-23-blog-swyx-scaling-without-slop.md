# Scaling without Slop (The State of Latent Space 2026)

**Blog** · swyx (Shawn Wang) · 2026-01-23 · [link](https://www.latent.space/p/2026)

**The take (attributed):** swyx argues the defining problem of the AI era is **"scaling without slop"** — increasing output quantity *and* quality at once — and that the answer is high-taste **curation** scaled across people, not cutting back human output.

**Stake:** Doubles as the "State of Latent Space" — the slop thesis positions his curated, human-edited media as the antidote to the AI-generated content flood, i.e. it sells his own product. Lower agent-research density than his other essays; included for the thesis, not for technical claims.

## Argument
- His AIE CODE keynote "declared War on Slop"; "Slop" then became word of the year (Merriam-Webster, the Economist). The reaction was to "performative productivity porn" in AI coding and AI ripping "the authenticity from creation" — 20% of YouTube, the App Store, maintainer workload.
- Pushes back on the obvious responses (reject all AI content / cut quantity / "eat the steak") as "defeatist": "plenty of Slop has been made by humans... AI does not have a monopoly on making Slop," but AI makes thoughtless output trivially scalable. Cutting your own output just lets AI slop "overwhelm you... by sheer brute force."
- "The central problem to solve is changing the **slope** of slop, not giving up on humans." Applies beyond media to "any act of creation."
- On execution: AI Engineer scaled 1→4→7 events with viewership 10x'ing *and* quality rising; Latent Space "fell behind on writing" (the [[2025-11-18-blog-swyx-agent-labs-thesis|Agent Labs Thesis]] underperformed the AI Engineer Reading List). The 2026 plan: more shows/hosts, more formats, more writing — "build a platform clearly bigger than me."
- "There is no school for curators... You have to have a point of view, you have to know your audience better than they know themselves."

## Why it matters / where it cuts
The "scaling without slop" framing is a sharp lens on the quality-vs-quantity problem every AI-augmented team now faces (and a useful counter to naive "10x output" hype). Less load-bearing for the radar's agent research than his other essays — captured mainly to round out swyx's 2026 voice and his taste/curation worldview, which informs how he frames the whole AI Engineering field.
