# Agentic AI in Healthcare & Medicine: A Seven-Dimensional Taxonomy for Empirical Evaluation of LLM-based Agents

**SURVEY** · arXiv [2602.04813v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04813v1) · 2026-02-04 · Shubham Vatsal, Harsh Dubey, Aditi Singh

**Why it matters:** A sharp, quantified view of what clinical agents can and can't yet do — and the safety/adaptation gaps regulators will care about.

## What it surveys
Agentic AI in healthcare/medicine, reviewing 49 studies against a structured frame.

## Framework / taxonomy
A seven-dimensional taxonomy (Cognitive Capabilities, Knowledge Management, Interaction Patterns, Adaptation & Learning, Safety & Ethics, Framework Typology, Core Tasks) with 29 sub-dimensions and a Fully/Partially/Not-Implemented labeling rubric.

## Notable points
- Clear capability asymmetries: external knowledge integration common (~76%), but drift detection (~98%) and event-triggered activation (~92%) almost absent.
- Multi-agent design dominant (~82%) but orchestration layers mostly partial.
- Action/discovery tasks (treatment planning ~59% not implemented) lag information tasks (QA, decision support).
