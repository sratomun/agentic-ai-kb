# Characterizing Faults in Agentic AI: A Taxonomy of Types, Symptoms, and Root Causes

**SURVEY** · arXiv [2603.06847v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06847v2) · 2026-03-06 · Mehil B Shah, Mohammad Mehdi Morovati, Mohammad Masudur Rahman, Foutse Khomh

**Why it matters:** The empirical backbone for the [[agent-reliability]] hub — a real catalog of how agents break in production and why.

## What it surveys
An empirical fault taxonomy for agentic AI, grounded in 13,602 issues/PRs across 40 repos (385 faults analyzed, validated with 145 practitioners).

## Framework / taxonomy
34 fault types across four architectural dimensions; faults manifest as failures in structured-output interpretation, tool calls, runtime execution, and exception handling, with root causes in data-schema mismatches, dependency drift, state-management complexity, and model-interface instability.

## Notable points
- Association-rule mining shows recurring cross-component fault propagation.
- Empirically grounded (real repos + developer validation), not speculative.
- Practitioners flagged multi-agent coordination and observability as refinement areas.
