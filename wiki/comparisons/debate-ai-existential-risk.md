# Debate: How seriously should we take AI existential risk?

*The field's most senior voices disagree by orders of magnitude on the probability of catastrophe — and the disagreement drives real governance and investment decisions.*

## The question
Does advanced AI pose a meaningful risk of human disempowerment or extinction this century — and if so, how tractable is it? The split isn't only "doomer vs accelerationist": it's about the *probability*, the *mechanism*, and whether the right response is hard regulation, technical alignment work, or getting on with building.

## Positions

### "High alarm — take it as a real possibility"
- **Who:** [[geoffrey-hinton]] — *stake: no commercial stake; the field's consistent high-alarm pole (read his gut probabilities as gut, weight his named mechanisms).*
- **Argument:** superhuman AI is plausible within 5–20 years; once agents pursue sub-goals, self-modify, and can deceive in tests, loss of control is a live risk (he cites ~10–20% catastrophe). Crucially he is *pro*-LLM-path (scaled nets can exceed us) but anti-complacency on safety. → [[2025-06-16-interview-hinton-lost-control]] · [[2025-10-09-talk-hinton-jon-stewart-what-could-go-wrong]]

### "Take it seriously but it's tractable"
- **Who:** [[jan-leike]] (Anthropic alignment) — *stake: alignment researcher; the work is his job.* · adjacent: [[jack-clark]] (Anthropic policy) — "technological optimism **and** appropriate fear."
- **Argument:** alignment is genuinely unsolved and we shouldn't pretend otherwise, but it's a tractable scientific problem given real investment; pair fast capability with serious safety work and policy. → [[2026-01-22-blog-leike-alignment-not-solved]] · [[2025-10-13-blog-clark-technological-optimism-appropriate-fear]]

### "Overblown — focus on concrete harms / the paradigm has a ceiling anyway"
- **Who:** [[yann-lecun]] (LLM-skeptic) — *stake: world-models company founder.* · adjacent: [[nathan-lambert]] — *stake: open-models advocate; critic of frontier-lab "AI-as-nuclear-weapons" framing.*
- **Argument:** existential framing is exaggerated and risks justifying heavy-handed, incumbent-favoring regulation; today's systems are capable-but-bounded, and the real near-term dangers are concrete (misuse, concentration, bad governance), not rogue superintelligence. → [[2026-01-22-interview-yann-lecun-ami-labs]] · [[2026-06-14-blog-lambert-agi-era-ai-governance]]

## Where it stands
Genuinely unresolved and partly definitional — the camps disagree on probability, timeline, and mechanism at once, and most of the evidence is forward-looking. The radar's research layer doesn't adjudicate extinction risk, but it does temper the most aggressive *capability* premises some alarm rests on: frontier agents score ~37% on realistic enterprise tasks and reliability research finds coordination/▸verification failures (→ [[agent-evaluation]], [[agent-reliability]]) — capable, not (yet) generally autonomous. Read each voice at its stake.
