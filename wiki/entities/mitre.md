# MITRE

*The not-for-profit behind ATT&CK — and now [[mitre-atlas|ATLAS]], the adversary-technique knowledge base for attacks on AI systems.*

## What it is
MITRE operates US federally funded R&D centers and curates the cybersecurity community's canonical adversary-behavior knowledge bases. For AI it maintains [[mitre-atlas|ATLAS]] (Adversarial Threat Landscape for AI Systems): a living matrix of 16 tactics and 84 techniques grounded in real-world red-team observations, structured as the AI-specific sibling of ATT&CK.

## Why it matters
MITRE is the *adversary-taxonomy* anchor of the [[security-standards|standards layer]]. Where [[nist]] organizes risk and [[owasp]] enumerates vulnerabilities, ATLAS describes how attacks actually unfold, tactic by tactic — which is exactly the framing a SOC or red team already lives in via ATT&CK. It's the cleanest bridge for getting an existing cyber org to reason about agentic threats without learning a new mental model.

## Relationships
- maintains [[mitre-atlas]] (and ATT&CK, its IT-security parent)
- complements [[nist]] (risk management), [[owasp]] (vulnerability checklist), [[cloud-security-alliance]] (threat modeling)
- part of the [[security-standards]] layer for [[agent-security]]
- techniques map to attack classes incl. [[tool-description-poisoning]], prompt injection, model evasion/extraction
