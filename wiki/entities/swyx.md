# swyx (Shawn Wang)

*Founder of the AI Engineer conferences and the Latent Space newsletter/podcast — the movement-builder who named the "AI Engineer" and "Agent Engineering" disciplines and is the field's leading curator of where applied-AI value is moving.*

## Who they are
Shawn "swyx" Wang is a developer-experience leader turned AI media founder. He runs **AI Engineer** (the ai.engineer conference series — NYC, World's Fair, Paris, CODE, and AIE Europe) and **Latent Space** (newsletter + top-ranked AI podcast, co-hosted with Alessio Fanelli), founded **Smol AI** (which produces the AI News daily newsletter), and contributes to coding-agent evaluation at **Cognition**. Background in devtools at AWS, Two Sigma, Netlify, Temporal, and Airbyte. He writes from a practitioner/curator/movement-builder lens, not a researcher's.

**Stake:** His business *is* the "AI Engineer / Agent Engineering" discipline — he profits (tickets, subscriptions, brand) from the application layer being taken seriously. Read his optimism about "Agent Labs" and the builder layer at that discount; he is explicit that he won't take vendor pitches and is investor-backed but "will never sell out."

## What they argue (recurring stances)
- **"AI Engineer" is a real discipline**, distinct from ML research — applied builders who *wield* foundation models via API will outnumber and out-ship researchers at the product layer. → [[2023-06-30-blog-swyx-rise-of-the-ai-engineer]]
- **Define agents descriptively, not prescriptively** — distills 250+ definitions into six elements (IMPACT: Intent, Memory, Planning, Authority, Control flow, Tools). "You can FEEL when an agent forgets one of these." → [[2025-03-24-blog-swyx-agent-engineering]]
- **[[mcp|MCP]] won on adoption mechanics, not technical merit** — AI-native standard + trusted big backer + proven base (LSP). Standards only have value through adoption. → [[2025-03-10-blog-swyx-why-mcp-won]]
- **Value is moving to the application/"Agent Lab" layer** — companies that research-and-sell *agents* (Cursor, Cognition, Sierra) over models; the pretraining→RL shift and "the Model Selector is a Lie" favor agent-as-system builders. → [[2025-11-18-blog-swyx-agent-labs-thesis]]
- **Pro-autonomy, but Bitter-Lesson-aware** — Agent Engineering / autonomy is "the point," yet he credits that workflows win short-term until the next intelligence jump steamrolls them. → [[debate-agents-vs-workflows]]
- **"Scaling without slop"** — the era's core problem is raising quantity *and* quality via scaled high-taste curation, not cutting human output. → [[2026-01-23-blog-swyx-scaling-without-slop]]
