# NIST

*US federal standards body that authored the [[nist-ai-rmf|AI Risk Management Framework]] — the de-facto reference for how to govern AI risk in the enterprise.*

## What it is
The National Institute of Standards and Technology, a non-regulatory US Commerce agency. In AI it owns the voluntary [[nist-ai-rmf|AI RMF (AI 100-1)]] and its [[nist-ai-rmf|Generative AI Profile (AI 600-1, Jul 2024)]], and is developing an Agentic Profile. Standards-setting, not enforcement — but its language is what regulators, auditors, and procurement teams reach for.

## Why it matters
NIST is the *risk-management* anchor of the [[security-standards|standards layer]]: it tells an org how to organize accountability (Govern/Map/Measure/Manage) rather than cataloguing specific attacks. For a cyber team, mapping controls to NIST functions is what makes an agent-security program legible to the board and to compliance. It already surfaces in the [[2026-06-21-us-federal-ai-framework|US federal AI posture]] (NIST/Commerce in the covered-frontier-model benchmarking loop) and the [[governance-gap|enterprise governance gap]].
