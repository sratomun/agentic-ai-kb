# ACP (Agent Communication Protocol)

*A proposed standard for federated, decentralized agent-to-agent interaction with identity verification, semantic intent mapping, and automated SLAs under a zero-trust posture.*

## What it is
ACP proposes a standardized cross-platform framework for agent-to-agent coordination: decentralized identity verification, semantic intent mapping, and automated service-level agreements, all under a zero-trust posture. It builds on [[mcp]] for local context and targets the decentralized-interaction gap that [[a2a-protocol|A2A]] also addresses.

## Why it matters
ACP's zero-trust and SLA posture is what enterprise compliance requires before letting agents span organizational boundaries — contracts, identity, and accountability baked into the protocol layer. The 'when, not if' requirement for regulated industries operating multi-org agent workflows.
