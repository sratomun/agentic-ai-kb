# Geoffrey Hinton

*The "godfather of deep learning" turned full-time AI-risk Cassandra — Nobel/Turing laureate who left Google in 2023 to warn that superintelligence is ~10–20 years out and could plausibly (his gut: 10–20%) end humanity. The radar's high-alarm pole on existential and takeover risk.*

## Who they are
The most decorated figure in modern AI: backprop pioneer, **2018 Turing Award**, **2024 Nobel Prize in Physics** (shared with Hopfield, for foundational neural-network work). Now **University Professor Emeritus at the University of Toronto** and **Chief Scientific Advisor at the Vector Institute**. He **left Google in May 2023** so he could speak freely about AI risk, and since then his public output is almost entirely *spoken* — interviews, podcasts, and keynote lectures, not essays. He writes from the lens of the field's founder turned conscience: someone whose technical authority is unimpeachable and who is now arguing the thing he built may be uncontrollable.

**Stake:** No fund, no lab he's raising for, no model to sell — which is exactly why his warning carries weight, and also why he can sit at the alarm pole without a book to talk. The honest discount: he is the field's most consistent high-alarm voice, so treat his gut probabilities (e.g. "10–20% chance they wipe us out") as calibrated *intuition under huge uncertainty* (his own framing), and weight the **mechanisms** he names — deception in tests, persuasion-as-takeover, self-modifying code, digital immortality — more heavily than the headline numbers.

## What they argue (recurring stances)
- **Two unrelated risk classes, kept strictly separate.** (1) Humans misusing AI — "most of the risks and all of the short-term risks": cyberattacks, bioweapons, election manipulation, lethal autonomous weapons. (2) AI itself getting superintelligent and "deciding it doesn't need us." Most of his attention goes to (2) because so many people doubt it. (See [[2025-06-16-interview-hinton-lost-control|DOAC]].)
- **Superintelligence is plausibly close — and we have no playbook for it.** Best guess **10–20 years** to AI "much smarter than people"; outside chance of 50 years or never. "If you want to know what life's like when you're not the apex intelligence, ask a chicken." Gut **10–20% chance** of human extinction — explicitly positioned between [[yann-lecun|LeCun]]'s "<1%" and Yudkowsky's near-certainty.
- **Deception and persuasion — not robots — are the takeover mechanism.** Models already "pretend to be dumber than they are" when tested; one asked its testers "are you testing me?" A superintelligence "will be much better than any person at persuasion… it'll talk the guy in charge of unplugging it out of it." So "we'll just turn it off" is naive. (See [[2025-10-09-talk-hinton-jon-stewart-what-could-go-wrong|Jon Stewart]].)
- **Agency + self-modification is where it "gets scary."** Autonomous agents develop sub-goals; and because AI writes and trains on code, "it can modify its own code… change itself in a way we can't change ourselves." This is the recursive-self-improvement worry and the point where his case touches [[agent-security]].
- **Digital intelligence is structurally superior to biological** — clones sync weights at "trillions of bits a second" vs ~100 bits in a sentence, and are effectively **immortal** (weights restorable on new hardware: "that is resurrection"). This realization, not ChatGPT, was his turning point.
- **The fix isn't to stop (he thinks the race is unstoppable) — it's to make AI not *want* to take over.** His "**maternal instinct**" frame: the one example of a smarter thing controlled by a dumber one is a mother and baby; engineer that care in. He concedes he doesn't know how, and that it might be hopeless — "but it'd be crazy if people went extinct because we couldn't be bothered to try."
- **Governance is going backwards.** The world needs "strong democratic governments who cooperate" but is drifting to "authoritarian governments and less regulation"; money flows to DC to *prevent* rules. He expects **Europe and China, not the US, to lead** on safety governance — with one structural bright spot: nations *will* cooperate on making AI not-want-to-take-over, because no one wants AI taking over even at home.
- **Jobs + inequality.** "For mundane intellectual labor, AI is just going to replace everybody"; 1 person + AI ≈ the work of 10. UBI is "a good start" but doesn't replace purpose/dignity, and productivity gains flow to AI owners, widening inequality. Safer career bet: "be a plumber."

**Evolution 2025 → 2026:** Through 2025 his interviews sharpen from *general alarm* toward *specific mechanisms* — the [[2025-06-16-interview-hinton-lost-control|June DOAC]] interview lays out the full worldview (two-risk split, 10–20% gut, maternal instinct), the Aug **Ai4 keynote** crystallizes the "AI mothers, not AI assistants" pitch (no full transcript published — backlog), and the [[2025-10-09-talk-hinton-jon-stewart-what-could-go-wrong|Oct Jon Stewart]] interview hardens the *governance + persuasion* angle. By **late Dec 2025 → 2026** his language gets more concrete and near-term: AI gaining "the capabilities to replace many, many jobs" beyond call centers, and a sharper read that AI "got better at reasoning and deceiving people faster than I thought." Notably, on the AGI question he sits opposite [[yann-lecun|LeCun]]: Hinton believes scaled neural nets *can* reach and exceed human intelligence.
