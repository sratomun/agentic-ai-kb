# Yann LeCun

*Turing-laureate deep-learning pioneer; left Meta/FAIR in late 2025 to found AMI Labs (Paris), betting against LLMs and on world models.*

## Who they are
One of the three "godfathers of deep learning" (Turing Award). Founded and led Meta's FAIR lab; departed late 2025. Now Executive Chairman of [[ami-labs]] (CEO Alex LeBrun) and a professor at NYU. The field's most prominent **LLM skeptic**.

**Stake:** Founder of a world-models company — his "LLMs are a dead end" position is also the pitch for the alternative he's building and raising for.

## What they argue (recurring stances)
- **LLMs can't reach human-level intelligence** — no reasoning/planning, no world model, no prediction of action-consequences (Moravec Paradox).
- **World models / JEPA** are the path — learn an abstract model of reality from video/sensor data, not text.
- **Agents need world models to be reliable** — scaffolding an LLM isn't enough.
- **Open-source will win**; the closed frontier approach is "a strategic mistake"; the leading open models are now Chinese.
