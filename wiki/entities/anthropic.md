# Anthropic

*The AI safety company behind Claude — whose models and governance decisions directly affect the exec's access and enterprise AI choices.*

## What it is
Anthropic is an AI safety-focused lab founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers, and the maker of the Claude model family. It originated the Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Constitutional AI alignment method. In June 2026, under a US government export-control directive, Anthropic disabled **Claude Fable 5** and **Claude Mythos 5** for all foreign nationals — the first time export controls were applied to an AI model itself (not chips). Anthropic publicly disagreed with the directive; lower-tier models (incl. Claude Opus 4.8) remain unaffected. → [[2026-06-21-frontier-model-export-controls]]

## Why it matters
Anthropic's decisions are direct operational dependencies for the exec: Claude is his primary model, MCP shapes tool-integration architecture, and the 2026 export-control episode showed that model access is now a geopolitical lever. From Brazil, top-tier Claude models may be gated — plan around Opus-tier access and watch for further fragmentation. See also: [[frontier-model-governance]], [[agentic-ai]]
