# Jason Wei

*The researcher who coined chain-of-thought prompting and named "emergent abilities," now one of the clearest written voices on **why verifiability is the master variable** of the reasoning-model era — articulating the worldview behind RL-on-verifiable-rewards from inside the frontier labs that ship it.*

## Who they are
Reasoning/RL research scientist at **Meta Superintelligence Labs** (joined July 2025). Previously at **OpenAI** (2023–2025), where he worked on reasoning models and **deep research**; before that at **Google Brain**, where he authored the **chain-of-thought prompting** paper and the **emergent abilities** paper — two of the most-cited results in the LLM-reasoning canon. He writes short, sharp essays at jasonwei.net (a "Blog" of conceptual pieces plus a "Thoughts" stream), with a parallel high-reach presence on X. His register is the practitioner-theorist: distilling a hard-won research intuition into one portable idea per post.

**Stake:** Frontier-lab researcher who helped *create* the reasoning/RL wave and now builds on it — so he talks the book on **RL with verifiable rewards** ([[rlvr|RLVR]]) being the path forward. His signature claim — "all tasks that are possible to solve and easy to verify will be solved by AI" — is both an honest research thesis and a justification for the program he works on. Discount the sweep of his "anything measurable will be solved" optimism; weight his RL, eval, and reasoning reads heavily — it's his home turf, and the ideas have held up.

## What they argue (recurring stances)
- **Verifiability is the master variable.** "Verifier's rule": the ease of training AI on a task is proportional to how cheaply and objectively a solution can be verified — because verifiability ≡ the ability to build an RL environment. ([[2025-07-15-blog-wei-asymmetry-of-verification|Asymmetry of verification and verifier's rule]].)
- **Expect a jagged frontier.** Because verifiable tasks are so much easier to train, AI will be "much smarter at verifiable tasks" — superhuman at code/math, shaky on fuzzy, hard-to-check work. (Same post.)
- **On-policy beats imitation.** RL works because the model learns from its own trajectories, not human demonstrations; "beating the teacher requires walking your own path." ([[2025-07-15-blog-wei-life-lessons-from-rl|Life lessons from RL]].)
- **AI research is a max-performance domain.** Frontier research rewards spiky genius — one seminal work outweighs incompetence everywhere else; "the metric is the best five works in your career, not the average," so interviews mis-measure researchers. ([[2025-05-29-blog-wei-ai-research-max-performance-domain|AI research is a max-performance domain]].)
- **AlphaEvolve is the proof of concept** that ruthless guess-and-check against a verifiable objective already yields real scientific/operational gains. (Asymmetry post.)

**Evolution 2025 → 2026:** Early-to-mid 2025 his writing is **insider culture/craft** — how frontier research actually works (max-performance domains, dopamine cycles) and how he runs his own research (on-policy, ablations on deep research). By July 2025, as he moves from OpenAI to Meta Superintelligence Labs, he crystallizes the **conceptual thesis of the whole RL-reasoning era** in the verifier's-rule essay — the single idea that now gets cited across the field as the explanation for both the speed of the reasoning wave and its jagged shape. The arc is from "how the labs work" to "the law that governs what they'll automate next."
