# Ethan Mollick

*Wharton professor and author of Co-Intelligence who writes the widely-read "One Useful Thing" — the radar's clearest voice on real-world AI/agent **adoption**: what crossed the capability line, how orgs should use it, and how work changes.*

## Who they are
Associate professor at **The Wharton School (University of Pennsylvania)**, studying the effects of AI on work, entrepreneurship, and education; co-director of Wharton's **Generative AI Labs**. Author of the NYT-bestselling **Co-Intelligence** (2024) with a follow-up, **Co-Existence**, due Oct 2026. He writes from a **practitioner/educator's seat**, not a researcher's bench: he runs hands-on demos on the latest models (often with early access), teaches MBAs to build with agents, and translates frontier capability into "what should a leader or knowledge worker actually do." His coinage of the **"Jagged Frontier"** (AI is superhuman at some tasks, hopeless at mundane others) is his most-cited frame.

**Stake:** Academic + bestselling author — no fund or lab equity, but he is reputationally and commercially invested in AI being *useful*, and he is one of the most prominent **adoption-optimists** in the discourse. Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all court him with early access; his early-2026 writing in particular leans notably pro-Anthropic. Weight his model demos and his org/work framing; discount the optimism and the "this is amazing" register.

## What they argue (recurring stances)
- **Agents crossed the "real work" threshold in late 2025 — but the unit is *tasks, not jobs*.** AI doing some tasks "shifts what I do," it doesn't replace the job while AI stays jagged. ([[2025-09-29-blog-mollick-real-ai-agents-and-real-work|Real AI Agents and Real Work]].)
- **The capability jump came from the *harness*, not just the model.** Self-correcting models + an agentic harness (compacting, [[agent-skills|skills]], subagents, [[acp-protocol|MCP]]) are what made coding agents suddenly run for hours unattended. ([[2026-01-07-blog-mollick-claude-code-and-what-comes-next|Claude Code and What Comes Next]].)
- **Coding agents are *general-purpose* tools** — "everything you do on a computer is, ultimately, code," so a coding agent can do dashboards, slides, research, browser tasks. Don't dismiss them as programmer tools. ([[2025-11-18-blog-mollick-three-years-from-gpt-3-to-gemini|Three Years from GPT-3 to Gemini 3]].)
- **Thriving with agents is a *management* problem: "delegation is the new prompting."** As the model gets capable, the binding constraint moves to the human — scoping, delegating, and evaluating ("management 101"). "What's scarce is knowing what to ask for." ([[2026-01-27-blog-mollick-management-as-ai-superpower|Management as AI superpower]].)
- **Choose AI along three axes — Models, Apps, Harnesses — and the app/harness now matter more than the model.** Practical evaluation from a user's seat: pay for the advanced model, pick it manually, mind the harness gap. ([[2026-02-18-blog-mollick-guide-to-which-ai-agentic-era|A Guide to Which AI to Use in the Agentic Era]].)
- **Human-in-the-loop is evolving from "fixes AI mistakes" to "directs AI work."** The errors that remain are increasingly *human-like judgement calls*, not hallucinations.
- **The org-level risk isn't only job loss — it's slop at scale** ("17 PowerPoints"). Use judgement on *what's worth doing*, not just what can be done.

**Evolution 2025 → 2026:** Early 2025 he's tracking the **reasoning/agentic unlock from a capability angle** ([[2025-04-20-blog-mollick-jagged-agi|"Jagged AGI"]], Prophecies of the Flood, the Cybernetic Teammate field experiment). Through mid–late 2025 he turns to **demonstrating agents doing real, economically valuable work** (Real AI Agents, GPT-5, On Working with Wizards). By 2026 his lens is squarely **adoption and org design** — the harness explainer (Claude Code), the delegation/management thesis, the Models/Apps/Harnesses buyer's guide — i.e. from "look what it can do" toward "here's how to run a workforce of agents," ending the "Co-Intelligence" framing as he moves to "Co-Existence."
