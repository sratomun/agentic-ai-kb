# RAW — AI research is a max-performance domain (Jason Wei, jasonwei.net)
Source: https://www.jasonwei.net/blog/ai-research-is-a-max-performance-domain · 2025-05-29 · captured 2026-06-22 · channel: blog

## Thesis
"A recent clarity that I gained is viewing AI research as a 'max-performance domain', which means that you can be world-class by being very good at only one part of your job."
- As long as you create seminal impact (train the best model, start a new paradigm, create widely adopted benchmarks), incompetence at adjacent skills doesn't matter.
- "I have seen game-changing AI researchers have horrendous presentation skills, terrible political awareness, and who never think about their career progression. Heck, I even know a top AI researcher who probably wouldn't pass a basic coding interview. But it doesn't matter."

## Consistency doesn't matter
- "An AI researcher can have tens of failed projects per year and still be successful if they produce a seminal work every few years. The metric is the best five works in your career, not the average."

## Danger: over-weighting role models
- "A dangerous thing in max-performance domains is placing too much emphasis on role models." You don't know whether you're mimicking the good characteristics. A top researcher can make a bad political move that works out for them because of who they are, or make a bold unsubstantiated statement and expect others to listen — but if anyone else did the same the outcome would be opposite.

## Exponential upside, little downside
- "Another way to view max-performance domains is that they have exponential upside and very little downside. That's why interviews are especially useless in domains like AI research, because they tend to severely punish mistakes and don't capture exponential value."
- "An RL expert doesn't need to know how SVMs work… A top AI infra engineer might lack basic knowledge about post-training data practices."

## Broader framing
- "In my view it's a luxury to work in a max-performance domain. Failure is allowed and stress is usually self-imposed."
- "A thousand years ago, very few humans worked in max-performance domains, but now the opportunity is more available. Technology may have played a role… and with the progression of AI, hopefully more of humanity can move into max-performance domains."
- Counter-example (non-max-performance domain): any career requiring both strengths AND essentially no weaknesses — a soccer defender can cost the team the game with a single mistake; a pianist must master all parts of a concerto.
