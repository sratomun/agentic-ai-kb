# RAW — Yann LeCun on AMI Labs (MIT Technology Review interview)

Source: https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/22/1131661/yann-lecuns-new-venture-ami-labs/
Author/outlet: Caiwei Chen, MIT Technology Review · 2026-01-22 · exclusive interview · captured 2026-06-22
Channel: interview (LeCun's own quotes)

Verified facts:
- LeCun left Meta (was Chief Scientist of FAIR, which he founded). Now Executive Chairman of
  **AMI Labs** (Advanced Machine Intelligence), HQ Paris; CEO Alex LeBrun. Keeps NYU professorship.
  (Other reporting: AMI raised ~$1.03B at $3.5B pre-money, announced ~March 2026.)

LeCun's argument (direct quotes / close paraphrase):
- On LLMs: "people have had this illusion, or delusion, that it is a matter of time until we can
  scale them up to having human-level intelligence, and that is simply false." LLMs are "limited
  to the discrete world of text. They can't truly reason or plan, because they lack a model of
  the world. They can't predict the consequences of their actions." Cites the Moravec Paradox.
- "We are going to have AI systems that have human-level intelligence, but they're not going to be
  built on LLMs… There are major conceptual breakthroughs that have to happen."
- LLMs become "the orchestrator in systems" — "LLM plus a lot of things" (perception, code), not pure LLMs.
- Solution: **world models** + **JEPA** (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture, created at Meta):
  "JEPA is not generative AI… learn an abstract representation of the world and make predictions in
  that abstract space, ignoring the details you can't predict… like a baby learning about gravity."
  Trained on video, audio, sensor data — not just text.
- On agents: "An agentic system that is supposed to take actions in the world cannot work reliably
  unless it has a world model to predict the consequences of its actions. Without it, the system
  will inevitably make mistakes." Key to domestic robots, Level-5 driving.
- On open vs closed: closed frontier approach (OpenAI/Anthropic) is "a strategic mistake"; "all
  leading open-source AI platforms are Chinese." AI will become a platform; platforms go open-source.
- On academia: "LLMs are now technology development, not research… Don't work on LLMs. There is no
  point… The breakthroughs are not going to come from scaling up LLMs." Most exciting world-model
  work is coming from academia, not big industrial labs.
- On humanoid robots (incl. Chinese): impressive demos are "planned in advance"; "nobody… knows how
  to make those robots smart enough to be useful" without world models + planning.

Stake: founder/Exec Chairman of AMI Labs — talking his own world-models, anti-LLM, open-source book.
