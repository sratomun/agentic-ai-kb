# RAW — "2026: This is AGI" (Sequoia Capital)

Source: https://sequoiacap.com/article/2026-this-is-agi/
Authors: Pat Grady and Sonya Huang (Sequoia Capital) · Published 2026-01-14 · captured 2026-06-22
Channel: technical blog (VC perspective essay)

Core argument (in their words / close paraphrase):
- "AGI is here, now." Long-horizon agents are *functionally* AGI, and 2026 is their year.
- Functional (not technical) definition: "AGI is the ability to figure things out." A human
  who can figure things out has baseline knowledge + reasoning + iteration. An AI now has the
  same three ingredients: knowledge (pre-training, the 2022 ChatGPT moment), reasoning
  (inference-time compute, o1 late 2024), and iteration (long-horizon agents — Claude Code and
  coding agents crossing a threshold "in the last few weeks").
- Two approaches both scaling: (1) reinforcement learning (research labs) to keep models on
  track longer; (2) agent harnesses (application layer) — scaffolding around model limits
  (memory hand-offs, compaction). Named: Manus, Claude Code, Factory Droids.
- METR exponential: AI's ability to complete long-horizon tasks doubling ~every 7 months;
  extrapolated to a full human-day of work by 2028, a year by 2034, a century by 2037.
- "So what": soon you can *hire* an agent. Examples cited as functioning specialists: OpenEvidence
  (medicine), Harvey (law), XBOW (cybersecurity/pentest), Traversal (SRE), Day AI (GTM), Juicebox
  (recruiting), Harmonic Aristotle (math), Ricursive (chip design); GPT-5.2 / Claude as AI researchers.
- Caveat acknowledged: "agents still fail. They hallucinate, lose context, and sometimes charge
  confidently down exactly the wrong path." But "failures are increasingly fixable."
- Framing for founders: 2023-24 apps were "talkers", 2026-27 will be "doers" — selling *work*, not software.

Stake: Pat Grady & Sonya Huang are Partners at Sequoia Capital — this is a thesis essay talking
the firm's AI/agents investment book.
