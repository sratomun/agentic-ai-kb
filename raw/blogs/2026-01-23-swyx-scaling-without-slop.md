At AIE Code, swyx's keynote "declared War on Slop." Inspired by Cognition's Codemaps and
Review, and a reaction to "performative productivity porn" ("Canadian Girlfriend AI
Coding"). "Slop" went on to be word of the year (Merriam-Webster, the Economist, American
Dialect Society).

The theme of fighting Slop resonates as "generative AI rips the authenticity from
creation" — open source maintainer workload, academic conference submissions, 20% of
YouTube videos, Instagram channels, the App Store, "slowly deadening the Internet."

Reasonable gut reactions to Slop (all "variously dissatisfying in their general defeatism"):
reject all AI content; severely cut back on quantity; or "just eat the steak."

swyx's core argument:
- "Plenty of Slop has been made by humans... AI does not have a monopoly on making Slop."
  But AI makes it easy to scale thoughtless output and harder to signal intent/effort/quality.
- "If your solution to AI slop basically means you cut back on your own human output, that
  doesn't solve the fact that AI slop will continue to far outpace human output." Cutting
  back just lets you be overwhelmed by brute force.
- "The most important problem in media now is **scaling without slop**, period, whether or
  not AI generated or entirely human-origin." Not just media — any act of creation is subject
  to the quality-quantity tradeoff.
- "The central problem to solve is changing the slope of slop, not giving up on humans."

## Scaling Latent Space
"A tougher beast." Strong podcast (top-3 AI podcast; ~1.5m all-time unique listeners) but
"fell behind on writing" — 2025's Agent Labs Thesis underperformed the AI Engineer Reading
List. Inspired by Dropout TV, Morning Brew, SemiAnalysis and a16z's "New Media" — wants to
"build a platform clearly bigger than me." 2026 game plan: more shows/hosts (AI for Science),
more formats (AINews daily), more writing.

## The Elements of Curation: Tuning High Taste
"The common thread among AIE and LS is that we have to curate very well, and then scale one
person's curation to many." Most visible output: "saying no a lot." "There is no school for
curators... You have to have a point of view, you have to know your audience better than they
know themselves." Funded by Substack subscribers + investors explicitly told "we will never
sell or sell out." "We just want to Make Good Shit at Scale."
