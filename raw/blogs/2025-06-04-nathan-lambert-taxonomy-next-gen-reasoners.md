# RAW — A taxonomy for next-generation reasoning models (Nathan Lambert, Interconnects)
Source: https://www.interconnects.ai/p/next-gen-reasoners · 2025-06-04 · captured 2026-06-22 · channel: blog

- Core taxonomy: four capabilities, in the order they should be solved — (1) SKILLS: solve self-contained problems; (2) CALIBRATION: judge a problem's difficulty and not overthink; (3) STRATEGY: choose the right high-level plan; (4) ABSTRACTION: break a strategy into solvable chunks. First two are native single-pass abilities; the latter two (planning) are "skills that are needed to build effective agents."
- Frame: "The first generation of reasoning models brought us inference-time scaling... The second generation... are going to bring us new types of agentic language modeling applications." Agentic traits are additive to gen-1, not present by default; can be partly bootstrapped with prompting but best results need training models to optimize for planning.
- Grounds in METR's "time horizon" chart (task length doubling ~every 7 months). Skills unlocked the bump through Claude Sonnet 3.7; "planning well will be the trait of models that make the leap from 1 to 4+ hours in 2026."
- SKILLS unlocked by scaling RLVR (first public confirmation: DeepSeek R1, training-time RL compute → performance). The skills race has ballooned an overthinking problem on easy questions.
- CALIBRATION currently offloaded to the user (model selectors, reasoning on/off, thinking-budget forcing, reasoning-effort sliders). RL loss functions are flexible enough to enable length control that instruction/preference tuning can't.
- On parallel/inference-time compute (o1-pro, Claude 4's parallel test-time sampling + internal scoring, Gemini Deep Think): "doing something very different than scaling the underlying RL — an added form of robustness or quality." Best thought of as "a tool that the agent can call," an arrow in the quiver during planning. Note: quality is often anti-correlated with rare tokens when reward models / majority voting are used.
- PLANNING = strategy + abstraction. STRATEGY: "with one autoregressive pass, pointing the stream of tokens in the wrong direction is often not recoverable." ABSTRACTION: break multi-day tasks into 1-2 minute inference steps (10-100K tokens). Closely related: context management (store a summary of what's done; skip completed tasks; retry after failure).
- o3 is the leading model in this paradigm — broad skills (math/code/search), some leading planning (Deep Research). But "when o3 is finding niche information... I attribute very little of that behavior to planning, but rather just the skill... of knowing to keep searching." o3 nearly solves search but fails at synthesis across a broad category (needs more advanced planning). Claude 4 / Claude Code leads on software-task planning — possibly because it's "taught to edit and revisit the plan many times while it is running."
- Path forward: planning likely needs manual data-annotation to bootstrap (like the Q* curation effort seeded reasoning traces), starting with cold-start SFT, before the final goal of "training agents end-to-end with RL on long-horizon, sparse tasks." Argues academia over-indexes on skills (math) and under-invests in planning. "The real race is towards building systems that people use... rather than pushing the models further into skills that aren't showing clear value."
Stake: researcher who coined RLVR in Tülu 3 (Ai2 at time of writing); open-models advocate; low commercial stake.
