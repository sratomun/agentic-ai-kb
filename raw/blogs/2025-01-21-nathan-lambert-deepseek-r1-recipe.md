# RAW — DeepSeek R1's recipe to replicate o1 and the future of reasoning LMs (Nathan Lambert, Interconnects)
Source: https://www.interconnects.ai/p/deepseek-r1-recipe-for-o1 · 2025-01-21 · captured 2026-06-22 · channel: blog

- Calls DeepSeek R1 the true open o1 replication: MIT-licensed flagship reasoning model trained via a 4-stage RL-heavy process, plus R1-Zero (RL-only from V3 base, no SFT), distilled small models, and a technical report. "A major transition point in the uncertainty in reasoning model research" — the missing seminal paper; reasoning research "now locked in."
- "New technical recipes normally aren't moats." Pricing collapse: o1 at $15/$60 per M tokens vs R1 at $0.55/$2.19 — ~10x cheaper; predicts a reasoning price war like the 2023 Mixtral inference race to the bottom.
- The 4-stage R1 recipe: (1) "cold-start" SFT on a few thousand filtered R1-Zero reasoning traces (improves readability + final perf); (2) large-scale RL on reasoning problems "until convergence"; (3) rejection sampling (800K completions: 600K reasoning + 200K general) to reintroduce general abilities; (4) final RL mixing verifiable rewards + RLHF preference reward models for helpfulness/harmlessness.
- R1-Zero: first open model trained with "large-scale RL without SFT as a preliminary step." Has reliability quirks (changes languages mid-reasoning). Shows the result he most wanted: TRAINING-TIME RL scaling (not just inference-time) correlated with eval performance, plus response length growing with training. Numbers look like they're "still going up."
- Three reward components in the reasoning RL: accuracy rewards (verifiable; the one that drives learning), format rewards (<think>/<answer> tags), language-consistency reward (slight perf degradation but better human prefs — "evaluation scores are not all that matters"). Uses GRPO (PPO with Monte Carlo advantage, no value model) — likely because it's the mature implementation in their infra (like OpenAI always using PPO).
- Scale context: R1 does 1000s of RL steps; Tülu 3 (his AI2 work) did 100s, biggest models ~50 steps. "Scaled-up RL relative to existing literature."
- Distillation finding (biggest open question): distilling strong models into small ones beats small-model RL, which needs "enormous computational power and may not even achieve" distillation perf; but advancing the intelligence frontier "may still require more powerful base models and larger-scale RL." Open question for 2025: "How small will... research drive advanced reasoning capabilities?"
- Notes the demise of process reward models (PRMs) and MCTS for o1-replication: "the winds of o1 replication have been blowing strongly away from any sort of explicit search... It really was, and is, a language model with the new reasoning behaviors coming from a lot of RL training."
- Calls to action: open RL datasets and verifier infra needed; AI2 building on Tülu 3 / open-instruct.
Stake: researcher who coined RLVR in Tülu 3 (Ai2 at time of writing); open-models advocate; low commercial stake.
