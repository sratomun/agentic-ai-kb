# RAW — Welcome to the AGI era of AI governance (Nathan Lambert, Interconnects)
Source: https://www.interconnects.ai/p/welcome-to-the-agi-era-of-ai-governance · 2026-06-14 · captured 2026-06-22 · channel: blog
NOTE: post is partly paywalled; capture covers the free portion.

- Frame: the US executive branch forcing Anthropic to suspend access (internal + external) to its Claude 5 Mythos/Fable models is "the starting gun of a new era in AI governance" — the AGI era, defined by AI agents that effectively complement human workers. Earlier era was the "ChatGPT era" (models that just answer questions); we're shifting "from answer inference to agentic inference" (cites Stratechery's "The Inference Shift").
- The saga: government reached out to Anthropic to suspend access to any foreign national / user abroad after a (narrow) jailbreak cybersecurity concern with the Fable model; the White House was tipped off by Amazon (Anthropic's largest partner).
- His likely-lasting opinions:
  1. An export ban on model weights is "a lasting, negative policy for the U.S." (open and closed).
  2. The cybersecurity concern was legitimate but narrow — shouldn't have triggered this; no model is jailbreak-proof and we must prep infra for a world where every global entity has models of this capability.
  3. "Anthropic's constant fear-mongering over the last few years has accelerated this moment." Comparing AI to nuclear weapons primed this style of governance — "some amount of Anthropic reaping what it has sowed."
  4. People (esp. open-source community) should NOT take a victory lap at the leading US AI company being subject to political attacks — economically unstable, could trigger recession / pop the AI bubble.
  5. The White House's actions are heavy-handed and politically inclined against Anthropic; there's "no domestic AI industry if foreign nationals cannot build with frontier AI in the U.S."
  6. Unclear why Amazon went to the White House directly; "if a private company tries to control the government the government will push back stronger."
- Bigger picture: a near-term world where "model releases are judged on vibes by an executive branch with minimal technical talent." This government "has internalized that we are in the AGI era... was not ready for it." Their original AI Action Plan reflected the ChatGPT era and was "mostly agreeable."
- Net stance: still on the side that risks have been exaggerated, "but the margin between the real risks of the latest AI systems and the language used to describe them has shrunk."
- Sovereign AI: Europe, the Middle East, likely China realizing they could be left without frontier AI; open-source community sees this as validation (businesses will want to control their own intelligence). But warns open-source advocates "wildly celebrating... aren't remotely ready for when the spotlight... turns its gaze upon them" — an aggressive action against an open model is "very likely" (3 months to 2 years out).
