# RAW — How to scale RL (Nathan Lambert, Interconnects)
Source: https://www.interconnects.ai/p/the-new-rl-scaling-laws · 2025-10-20 · captured 2026-06-22 · channel: blog

- Reviews the first definitive paper on scaling RL: "The Art of Scaling Reinforcement Learning Compute for LLMs" (Khatri & Madaan et al. 2025, "ScaleRL", Meta + collaborators). "The most covetable research." His AI2 reasoning team named their main Slack channel "scaling-rl" all of 2025 in anticipation.
- Two reasons scaling RL has been hard to copy for academics: (1) lack of stable RL training setups (many libraries maturing in parallel); (2) lack of compute for experimentation. Mirrors the progression of open Mixture-of-Experts models.
- Method intuition: take a base model, run a bit of RL, fit a sigmoid (constants A = peak/asymptotic accuracy, B = slope, C = compute), then predict the endpoint of a long run from early measurements. In the paper, curves fit over 1/4 of training compute predict the outcome of the remaining 3/4.
- KEY CAVEAT: these are NOT like pretraining scaling laws. Accuracy on RL training data is "a far more bounded measure than next token prediction." RL scaling laws are most useful for ABLATING DESIGN CHOICES, not pointing to something fundamental. Pretraining laws choose the config for a run you can't debug; RL laws are "more about ablating which algorithm you'll let run much longer." Scaling RL is "more like an art."
- Recurring meta-point: "Most of the key ideas are out there, but open labs tend to not have the resources to put them all together in the right configuration." Slow accumulation of knowledge needs organizational intensity small groups can't match.
- Algorithms blessed as important: truncated importance sampling (TIS), Group Sequence Policy Optimization (GSPO), CISPO (from MiniMax M1). Importance sampling re-weights advantage/reward by the log-ratio between the actor (e.g. vLLM generating) and learner (e.g. Transformers doing gradient updates) — "essential to getting modern RL infrastructure right" for numerical stability.
- Systems: PipelineRL (ServiceNow) as canonical reference for in-flight updates (changing weights mid-generation) + continuous batching (filling the RL batch over time) → 4X+ throughput over standard LLM RL. A 1-day vs 4-day feedback cycle changes the whole research setup. AI2's Open Instruct has both features.
- Eras of RL algos (per Ross Taylor): vanilla GRPO → DAPO (fixed clipping/advantage biases) → CISPO / TIS (sequence-level, actor-learner correction). On the "GRPO is far behind frontier labs" brouhaha: more accurate that *vanilla* GRPO is far behind; the set of tricks that works on your model and data is a well-kept secret. ScaleRL is "a major step in showing people how to bridge that gap."
- Open questions the paper leaves: impact of different data (uses Polaris 53K math set; harder data may become an open-method limitation); how to choose the right base model (bigger base models RL better — 17Bx16 MoE beat 8B dense using 1/6 the RL compute). Footnote: "very long horizon" RL for scientific discovery is still much further off.
