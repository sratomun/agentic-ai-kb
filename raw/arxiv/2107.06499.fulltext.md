# Full-text capture — arXiv:2107.06499 "Deduplicating Training Data Makes Language Models Better" (via alphaXiv analysis, ingested 2026-06-22)

Authors: Katherine Lee, Daphne Ippolito, Andrew Nystrom, Chiyuan Zhang, Douglas Eck, Chris Callison-Burch, Nicholas Carlini (Google Research, Brain Team; University of Pennsylvania).

## Motivation
Large training corpora (C4, Wiki-40B, LM1B, RealNews) contain pervasive duplication. This causes high rates of memorized output, inaccurate evaluation via train-test overlap, and inefficient training. The authors develop scalable deduplication methods and show concrete benefits.

## Methods
1. **EXACTSUBSTR (exact substring duplication):** concatenate all examples; build a suffix array (linear-time SA-IS, parallelized); find adjacent suffixes sharing a common prefix of >= **50 BPE tokens** as exact duplicate substrings and remove one occurrence. ~1000 CPU-hours (under 12 hours) for the 350GB C4 dataset.
2. **NEARDUP (approximate full-document matching):** represent each document by its 5-grams; MinHash signatures (k=9,000 smallest hashed n-grams) partitioned into r=450 buckets of b=20 hashes; candidate matches verified by edit similarity (1 − normalized edit distance) > **0.8**; cluster by connected components, retain one per cluster. Distributed implementation consumed ~41.5 kWh for English C4.

When duplicates spanned splits, a copy was kept in test/validation and removed from training to preserve evaluation integrity.

**Models:** 1.5B "XL" and 110M "base" decoder-only Transformers (GPT-2-like, built on the T5 codebase), trained on C4-ORIGINAL, C4-NEARDUP, C4-EXACTSUBSTR.

## Main Findings
**Pervasiveness of duplication:**
- NEARDUP near-duplicate example fractions: RealNews **13.63%**, LM1B **4.86%**, C4 **3.04%**, Wiki-40B **0.39%**.
- EXACTSUBSTR token removal: C4 **7.18%**, RealNews **19.4%**, LM1B **0.76%**.
- Web datasets: duplicates are mostly templated computer-generated text; news datasets: same articles across sites.

**Train-test leakage:** **4.6%** of C4's validation set and **14.4%** of RealNews's validation set had approximate duplicates in their training sets — inflating evaluation metrics.

**Reduced memorization:** For models trained on original C4, over **1%** of unprompted output tokens were exact 50-token substrings copied verbatim from training; this dropped by **10x** to ~**0.1%** for models trained on deduplicated C4. When prompted with duplicated training examples, the original-C4 model reproduced the ground-truth continuation over **40%** of the time vs ~**3–6%** for deduplicated models.

**Perplexity:** Models trained on deduplicated data achieved comparable or lower perplexity on external validation (LM1B, Wiki-40B) despite seeing less data; the XL NEARDUP model reduced Wiki-40B perplexity by almost **3 points**. Deduplicated models showed higher perplexity on duplicated validation examples (less overfitting to repeats).

**Efficiency:** C4-EXACTSUBSTR was 7.18% smaller (tokens); C4-NEARDUP 3.9% smaller (examples). Training XL on C4-NEARDUP saved ~5 hours of compute over two epochs on a 128-core TPU v3 pod slice.

**Existing models also affected:** Transformer-XL on LM1B had half the perplexity on near-duplicate vs unique validation examples; GROVER-Mega outputs were **1.38%** verbatim training tokens; GPT-Neo 1.3B over **5%**.

## Significance
Establishes deduplication as a fundamental data-quality step: better generalization, more accurate/trustworthy evaluation, training efficiency and sustainability, and reduced privacy risk from memorization. Deduplication code was released. Directly informs later pipelines (RefinedWeb, FineWeb, DCLM all dedup heavily).
