# Full-text capture — arXiv:2406.11794 "DataComp-LM (DCLM): In search of the next generation of training sets for language models" (via alphaXiv analysis, ingested 2026-06-22)

Authors: Jeffrey Li, Alex Fang, Georgios Smyrnis (shared first); 40+ contributors across U. Washington, UT Austin, Apple, AI2, etc.; Achal Dave, Ludwig Schmidt, Vaishaal Shankar (shared last).

## Objectives
Establish a standardized, large-scale testbed for controlled dataset experiments. Address the lack of controlled comparisons in data-curation research; promote transparency; provide a comprehensive resource and a strong public baseline.

## DCLM Components
- **DCLM-POOL:** standardized corpus of **240 trillion tokens** from Common Crawl (pre-2023 dumps). Text extracted with **resiliparse** (chosen over WET files and trafilatura — better downstream performance and **8x faster** than trafilatura).
- **Scale selection:** five compute scales, from 412M params / 8.2B tokens (400M-1x) up to 7B params / 276B tokens (7B-2x). High rank correlation of dataset performance across scales validates the multi-scale design.
- **Tracks:** Filtering (subset DCLM-POOL only) and Mixing (combine with external sources).
- **Standardized training:** decoder-only Transformer (GPT-2/Llama inspired) in the OpenLM framework.
- **Evaluation:** 53 downstream tasks via LLM-Foundry; metrics include MMLU 5-shot, CORE centered accuracy (22 low-variance tasks), EXTENDED centered accuracy (all 53).

## Building DCLM-BASELINE (Ablations)
- **Existing datasets:** RefinedWeb performed best among open datasets evaluated (C4, RefinedWeb, RedPajama, Dolma-V1); its heuristic filters were adopted as a starting point.
- **Deduplication:** MinHash+SuffixArray vs a modified Bloom filter (BFF) — comparable downstream performance; Bloom filter chosen for scalability.
- **Model-based quality filtering (most impactful step):** tested PageRank, SemDedup, BGE-embedding classifiers, AskLLM, perplexity, top-k logits. A **fastText binary classifier** consistently outperformed others. Training the fastText classifier on instruction-formatted positive examples (OpenHermes 2.5 + high-scoring r/ExplainLikeImFive posts) gave a **3.5 percentage-point lift on CORE** vs traditional sources like Wikipedia. A strict threshold (keep top **10%** of documents by score) was most effective. Different filtering models spanned a wide range — **35% to 44% MMLU** at the 7B scale.
- **Mixing:** combining DCLM-BASELINE with traditional "high-quality" sources (Wikipedia, books) surprisingly **hurt** performance.
- Human quality judgments had limited value (poor correlation with actual LM performance).

## Main Findings
- **DCLM-BASELINE 7B**, trained on **2.6 trillion tokens**, achieves **64% 5-shot MMLU** — a **6.6 percentage-point improvement over MAP-Neo** (previous open-data SOTA) while using **40% less compute**.
- Comparable to Mistral-7B-v0.3 (63% MMLU) and Llama 3 8B (66% MMLU) across 53 tasks, with **6.6x less compute than Llama 3 8B**.
- At 280B tokens, DCLM-BASELINE 7B beats Llama 2 7B by **5 percentage points MMLU** using **7x less compute**.
- Dataset rankings hold across scales; data improvements generalize across architectures (Transformer, Gemma-like, Mamba).

## Significance
First large-scale controlled testbed for LM training-data curation. Champions data-centric AI, democratizes research (better data → less compute), enhances transparency (open corpus, baseline, framework, evals), and provides actionable curation guidance (resiliparse extraction, fastText classifier with instruction-formatted positives).
