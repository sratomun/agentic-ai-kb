# Full-text capture — arXiv:2101.00027 "The Pile: An 800GB Dataset of Diverse Text for Language Modeling" (via alphaXiv, ingested 2026-06-22)

## Introduction

Language models have become increasingly powerful in recent years, but their capabilities depend heavily on the data they're trained on. EleutherAI, a decentralized research collective, created "The Pile" - an 800GB dataset specifically designed to train large-scale language models with diverse knowledge across domains.

The Pile addresses a critical challenge in language model training: the need for high-quality, diverse textual data that extends beyond the web-scraped content typically used. Rather than relying solely on Common Crawl data, which dominates many existing language model training sets, The Pile incorporates 22 diverse datasets spanning academic publications, code, books, and specialized professional texts.

This dataset represents a significant contribution to the AI research community by providing an openly accessible resource that enables the training of more capable and domain-knowledgeable language models.

## Dataset Composition

The Pile consists of 22 carefully curated datasets, totaling approximately 825 GB of English text. These components can be grouped into several categories:

1. **Academic and scientific texts**: PubMed Central (PMC) articles, ArXiv papers, PubMed abstracts, NIH ExPorter grant abstracts, PhilPapers philosophy papers
2. **Code and technical documentation**: GitHub code repositories, Stack Exchange posts, Ubuntu IRC chat logs
3. **Books and literature**: Books3 collection, BookCorpus2, Project Gutenberg (PG-19)
4. **Web and general content**: OpenWebText2, Pile-CC (filtered Common Crawl), English Wikipedia, HackerNews discussions
5. **Specialized professional text**: FreeLaw legal documents, USPTO patents, Enron Emails, EuroParl proceedings, DM Mathematics
6. **Miscellaneous**: YouTube subtitles, OpenSubtitles

The creators introduced 14 new datasets specifically for The Pile, making a significant contribution beyond simply aggregating existing resources. Each component was selected to enhance the dataset's coverage of domains that are typically underrepresented in web-scraped corpora.

## Research Objectives

The Pile was developed to address data scarcity, enhance data diversity, create a benchmark for evaluating LMs across diverse domains, demonstrate that a curated dataset could yield better performance than filtered web data alone, and promote reproducibility.

## Methodology

**Dataset Selection and Creation**: Domains important for LM training but underrepresented in existing datasets were identified; existing high-quality datasets were incorporated, and new datasets created where gaps remained.

**Common Crawl Filtering**: A significant contribution was Pile-CC, a filtered subset of Common Crawl using jusText for higher-quality extraction, removing boilerplate and preserving meaningful text.

**Quality Control**: Extensive filtering removed low-quality content, repetitive text, and machine-generated material; formatting, encoding, and normalization were addressed.

**Benchmarking Protocol**: Evaluation tested pretrained models (GPT-2 and GPT-3) and newly trained models on different components, using perplexity / bits-per-byte (BPB).

## Performance Results

1. **Struggles of Existing Models**: When evaluated on The Pile, both GPT-2 and GPT-3 showed significant performance gaps across components, particularly struggling with specialized domains like academic writing and technical documentation (e.g., DM Mathematics and Enron Emails present the greatest challenges in relative BPB).

2. **Improvements from Pile Training**: Models trained on The Pile significantly outperformed those trained on both raw Common Crawl and CC-100 (a filtered version) across all components of The Pile, demonstrating the value of diversity and quality over mere quantity.

3. **Domain-Specific Gains**: The most substantial improvements were observed in specialized domains like mathematics, code, and technical documentation. For example, a model trained on The Pile showed approximately 1.65 BPB improvement on the DM Mathematics component compared to Common Crawl-trained alternatives.

4. **Generalization Capabilities**: Pile-trained models demonstrated better zero-shot performance not only on in-distribution data but also on several external benchmarks.

## Scaling Properties

The researchers observed a consistent scaling law relating model size to perplexity (BPB) on The Pile: as model size increases, perplexity decreases according to a power law without signs of diminishing returns. Different components vary considerably in their "difficulty" for language models (mathematics and EuroParl proceedings pose greater challenges than general web text).

## Ethical Considerations

The authors examined the dataset for profanity, offensive language, and biased associations, finding varying levels across components (OpenSubtitles highest profanity proportion; academic sources minimal). They analyzed gender and religious associations and used both datasheet and data statement methodologies to document characteristics, limitations, and risks. They note models trained on The Pile may inherit biases present in the data.

## Significance and Impact

The Pile provides a large-scale, diverse dataset openly to the community, serves as a benchmark for evaluating model performance across diverse domains, contributes methodological advances in dataset construction/documentation/ethical analysis, introduces 14 new datasets, and provides empirical evidence for the value of dataset diversity in LM training (clear advantages over web-scraped alternatives).
