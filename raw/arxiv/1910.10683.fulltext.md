# Full-text capture — arXiv:1910.10683 "Exploring the Limits of Transfer Learning with a Unified Text-to-Text Transformer" (T5/C4) (via alphaXiv analysis, ingested 2026-06-22)

Authors: Colin Raffel, Noam Shazeer, Adam Roberts, Katherine Lee, Sharan Narang, Michael Matena, Yanqi Zhou, Wei Li, Peter J. Liu (Google). Published JMLR 2020.

## Objectives and Motivation
The paper explores the landscape of transfer learning techniques for NLP by introducing a unified text-to-text framework, and pushes the limits by scaling models and datasets beyond what had previously been considered. The goal is "not to propose new methods but instead to provide a comprehensive perspective on where the field stands." Data, models, and code were released.

## Methodology
**Unified text-to-text framework:** Every NLP task (classification, summarization, QA, translation, regression) is reframed as converting input text to output text, with task-specific prefixes (e.g., "translate English to German:", "summarize:"). All tasks trained with maximum likelihood / teacher forcing.

**Model architecture:** Encoder-decoder Transformer (Vaswani et al. 2017) with simplified LayerNorm (no additive bias) and relative position embeddings. Variants compared: standard encoder-decoder, shared-parameter encoder-decoder, fewer-layer encoder-decoder, decoder-only LM, decoder-only prefix LM.

**Pre-training dataset — Colossal Clean Crawled Corpus (C4):** Derived from Common Crawl with extensive cleaning heuristics: retain lines with terminal punctuation; discard pages with few sentences or lines with few words; remove "bad words," JavaScript mentions, "lorem ipsum," curly brackets (code), Wikipedia citation markers, boilerplate policy notices; deduplicate 3-sentence spans; language filter (English >0.99 probability). Base C4 dataset is about 750 GB. Dataset variants compared: C4, unfiltered C4, RealNews-like, WebText-like, Wikipedia, Wikipedia+TBC.

**Unsupervised objectives:** Baseline denoising objective: sample 15% of tokens, replace consecutive corrupted spans with a single sentinel token, predict original spans. Variants: prefix LM, BERT-style, deshuffling, MASS-style, drop-corrupted-tokens, varying corruption rates (10/15/25/50%), span corruption (avg span length 2/3/5/10).

**Pre-training scale:** Baseline pre-trained on 2^35 ≈ 34B tokens; final models pre-trained on ~1 trillion tokens.

**Model sizes:** Small (60M), Base (220M), Large (770M), 3B (2.8B), 11B (11B parameters).

**Downstream benchmarks:** GLUE (8 tasks), SuperGLUE (8 tasks), CNN/Daily Mail summarization, SQuAD QA, WMT En→De/Fr/Ro translation.

## Main Findings
- **Architecture:** Encoder-decoder Transformer generally outperformed decoder-only LM and prefix-LM for text-to-text tasks. Sharing encoder-decoder parameters matched the non-shared variant while halving parameter count.
- **Objectives:** Most denoising objectives performed similarly and beat plain LM and deshuffling. Replacing corrupted spans with a single sentinel and predicting only corrupted tokens was slightly better and more efficient. Corruption rate of 15% was a good balance.
- **Data:** Cleaning is crucial — unfiltered Common Crawl significantly degraded performance. In-domain pre-training boosts specific downstream tasks. Performance degraded when the pre-training dataset was too small and repeated excessively (e.g., thousands of times), suggesting memorization — motivating large datasets like C4.
- **Training strategies:** Fine-tuning all parameters beat adapter layers and gradual unfreezing. Multi-task pre-training + fine-tuning was comparable to unsupervised-only pre-training + fine-tuning.
- **Scaling:** More model size, more steps, or larger batch all helped and were largely complementary. Ensembling (especially independent models) gave significant gains.
- **State of the art:** Combining best choices with scale (up to 11B params, ~1 trillion tokens), T5 achieved SOTA on 18 of 24 tasks across GLUE, SuperGLUE, SQuAD, and CNN/Daily Mail. T5-11B nearly matched human performance on SuperGLUE.

## Significance
Consolidated transfer-learning knowledge into a unified framework, validated the text-to-text paradigm, introduced and open-sourced C4 (~750 GB), and confirmed the power of scale. C4 became a staple pre-training resource and the basis for later open-data work (FineWeb, DCLM, RefinedWeb comparisons).
