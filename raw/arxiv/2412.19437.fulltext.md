# DeepSeek-V3 Technical Report

DeepSeek-AI
research@deepseek.com
arXiv:2412.19437v2 [cs.CL] 18 Feb 2025

## Abstract

We present DeepSeek-V3, a strong Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) language model with 671B total
parameters with 37B activated for each token. To achieve efficient inference and cost-effective
training, DeepSeek-V3 adopts Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA) and DeepSeekMoE architec-
tures, which were thoroughly validated in DeepSeek-V2. Furthermore, DeepSeek-V3 pioneers
an auxiliary-loss-free strategy for load balancing and sets a multi-token prediction training
objective for stronger performance. We pre-train DeepSeek-V3 on 14.8 trillion diverse and
high-quality tokens, followed by Supervised Fine-Tuning and Reinforcement Learning stages to
fully harness its capabilities. Comprehensive evaluations reveal that DeepSeek-V3 outperforms
other open-source models and achieves performance comparable to leading closed-source
models. Despite its excellent performance, DeepSeek-V3 requires only 2.788M H800 GPU hours
for its full training. In addition, its training process is remarkably stable. Throughout the entire
training process, we did not experience any irrecoverable loss spikes or perform any rollbacks.
The model checkpoints are available at https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V3.

## 1. Introduction (key passages)

To further push the boundaries of open-source model capabilities, we scale up our models and
introduce DeepSeek-V3, a large Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) model with 671B parameters, of which
37B are activated for each token.

In terms of architecture, DeepSeek-V3 still adopts Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA) for efficient
inference and DeepSeekMoE for cost-effective training. These two architectures have been
validated in DeepSeek-V2. Beyond the basic architecture, we implement two additional strategies:
(1) DeepSeek-V3 pioneers an auxiliary-loss-free strategy for load balancing, minimizing the
adverse impact on model performance that arises from the effort to encourage load balancing;
(2) DeepSeek-V3 employs a multi-token prediction training objective, which we have observed to
enhance the overall performance on evaluation benchmarks.

In order to achieve efficient training, we support FP8 mixed precision training and implement
comprehensive optimizations for the training framework. We introduce an FP8 mixed precision
training framework and, for the first time, validate its effectiveness on an extremely large-scale
model. As for the training framework, we design the DualPipe algorithm for efficient pipeline
parallelism, which has fewer pipeline bubbles and hides most of the communication during training
through computation-communication overlap. We also develop efficient cross-node all-to-all
communication kernels to fully utilize InfiniBand (IB) and NVLink bandwidths.

During pre-training, we train DeepSeek-V3 on 14.8T high-quality and diverse tokens. The
pre-training process is remarkably stable. Throughout the entire training process, we did not
encounter any irrecoverable loss spikes or have to roll back. Next, we conduct a two-stage
context length extension for DeepSeek-V3. In the first stage, the maximum context length is
extended to 32K, and in the second stage, it is further extended to 128K. Following this, we
conduct post-training, including Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) and Reinforcement Learning (RL).
During the post-training stage, we distill the reasoning capability from the DeepSeek-R1 series
of models.

- On top of the efficient architecture of DeepSeek-V2, we pioneer an auxiliary-loss-free strategy
  for load balancing, which minimizes the performance degradation that arises from encouraging
  load balancing.
- We investigate a Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) objective and prove it beneficial to model

## 2. Architecture

The basic architecture of DeepSeek-V3 is within the Transformer framework, adopting MLA and
DeepSeekMoE, validated by DeepSeek-V2, with an additional auxiliary-loss-free load balancing
strategy for DeepSeekMoE.

### 2.1.1. Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA)
The core of MLA is the low-rank joint compression for attention keys and values to reduce
Key-Value (KV) cache during inference.

### 2.1.2. DeepSeekMoE with Auxiliary-Loss-Free Load Balancing
DeepSeek-V3 uses DeepSeekMoE with finer-grained experts and shared experts. It pioneers an
auxiliary-loss-free strategy: a bias term is added to each expert's affinity score to determine
top-K routing, and the bias is dynamically adjusted (increased for underloaded experts, decreased
for overloaded) to keep load balanced without auxiliary-loss interference.

### 2.2. Multi-Token Prediction (MTP)
DeepSeek-V3 sets a multi-token prediction training objective. For each prediction depth, a
cross-entropy loss is computed; the overall MTP loss is the average over depths scaled by weight λ.
During inference, MTP modules can be discarded (main model functions normally) or repurposed
for speculative decoding to improve generation latency.

## 3. Infrastructures

### 3.1. Compute Clusters
DeepSeek-V3 is trained on a cluster equipped with 2048 NVIDIA H800 GPUs. Each node in the
H800 cluster contains 8 GPUs connected by NVLink and NVSwitch within nodes. Across different
nodes, InfiniBand (IB) interconnects are utilized.

### 3.2. Training Framework — DualPipe
The DualPipe algorithm is designed for efficient pipeline parallelism with fewer pipeline bubbles
and computation-communication overlap. Efficient cross-node all-to-all communication kernels
fully utilize IB and NVLink bandwidths. Memory footprint is meticulously optimized, making it
possible to train DeepSeek-V3 without using costly tensor parallelism.

### 3.3. FP8 Training
We propose a mixed precision framework for FP8 training. Most compute-density operations
(GEMM) are conducted in FP8; key operations (embedding module, output head, MoE gating,
normalization operators, attention operators) are maintained in higher precision (BF16/FP32).
All three GEMMs of the Linear operator — Fprop (forward), Dgrad (activation backward), Wgrad
(weight backward) — are executed in FP8. This design theoretically doubles computational speed
compared with the original BF16 method.

A fine-grained quantization strategy is introduced: tile-wise grouping with 1×Nc elements
(activations: 1×128 tile, per token per 128 channels) or block-wise grouping with Nc×Nc elements
(weights: 128×128 block). Compared with the BF16 baseline, the relative loss error of the
FP8-training model remains consistently below 0.25%, well within the acceptable range of training
randomness.

Increasing Accumulation Precision: FP8 GEMM accumulation precision on NVIDIA H800 GPUs is
limited to ~14 bits. Promotion to CUDA Cores for FP32 accumulation at intervals of Nc = 128
elements (4 WGMMAs) is adopted. The E4M3 format is used on all tensors.

Low-precision optimizer states (AdamW first and second moments) are stored in BF16; master
weights and gradients are retained in FP32. Activations are cached in FP8 for the backward pass
(with E5M6 for sensitive activations).

## 4. Pre-Training

### 4.1. Data Construction
The tokenizer employs Byte-level BPE with an extended vocabulary of 128K tokens. A FIM
(Fill-in-Middle) strategy with the Prefix-Suffix-Middle (PSM) framework is applied at a rate of 0.1.

### 4.2. Hyper-Parameters

Model Hyper-Parameters: Number of Transformer layers = 61; hidden dimension = 7168. All
learnable parameters randomly initialized with standard deviation 0.006. In MLA: number of
attention heads n_h = 128, per-head dimension d_h = 128; KV compression dimension d_c = 512;
query compression dimension d'_c = 1536; decoupled queries/key per-head dimension = 64. All
FFNs except the first three layers are substituted with MoE layers. Each MoE layer consists of 1
shared expert and 256 routed experts, with intermediate hidden dimension of each expert = 2048.
Among the routed experts, 8 are activated for each token, and each token is sent to at most 4 nodes.
MTP depth D = 1 (each token predicts one additional token). Under this configuration, DeepSeek-V3
comprises 671B total parameters, of which 37B are activated for each token.

Training Hyper-Parameters: AdamW optimizer with β1 = 0.9, β2 = 0.95, weight_decay = 0.1.
Maximum sequence length 4K during pre-training; pre-trained on 14.8T tokens. Learning rate
linearly increased from 0 to 2.2×10^-4 during first 2K steps, kept constant until 10T tokens, then
decayed to 2.2×10^-5 over 4.3T tokens (cosine). Gradient clipping norm = 1.0. Batch size
gradually increased from 3072 to 15360 over first 469B tokens, then kept at 15360. For
auxiliary-loss-free load balancing, bias update speed γ = 0.001 for first 14.3T tokens, 0.0 for
remaining 500B. MTP loss weight λ = 0.3 for first 10T tokens, 0.1 for remaining 4.8T.

### 4.3. Long Context Extension
After pre-training, YaRN is applied for context extension in two additional training phases, each
comprising 1000 steps, expanding the context window from 4K to 32K and then to 128K. Phase 1:
sequence length 32K, batch size 1920. Phase 2: sequence length 128K, batch size 480. DeepSeek-V3
performs well on the "Needle In A Haystack" (NIAH) test across all context window lengths up to 128K.

## 5. Post-Training
Post-training includes Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) and Reinforcement Learning (RL, using Group
Relative Policy Optimization / GRPO), with reasoning capability distilled from the DeepSeek-R1
series of models.
