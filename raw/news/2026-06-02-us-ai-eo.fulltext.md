FULL TEXT (EO body, key sections):

Sec. 1 Purpose. Frames continued US AI leadership with light regulation; advanced AI introduces national-security considerations requiring coordinated agency action; "America First cybersecurity."

Sec. 2 Upgrading American Systems for Advanced AI (all within 30 days unless noted):
- Committee on National Security Systems to prioritize cyber defense of National Security Systems.
- Secretary of War to prioritize cyber defense of DoW systems.
- DHS/CISA (with OMB, APNSA, National Cyber Director) to issue Binding Operational Directives to expedite cyber defense of civilian federal systems, expand AI-enabled defensive tools, and facilitate access to cybersecurity tools — "including, where appropriate, covered frontier models" — for agencies, state/local authorities, and critical-infrastructure operators (rural hospitals, community banks, local utilities).
- Treasury (with NCD, NSA, CISA) to form an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse to coordinate vulnerability scanning, validation, and patch distribution.
- OMB to check federal grant funding for AI vulnerability detection; OPM to expand cyber hiring pathways (60 days).

Sec. 3 Secure Frontier Model Deployment (within 60 days). Treasury, NSA, CISA (with NCD, APST, NIST/Commerce) shall:
(a) develop and maintain a CLASSIFIED benchmarking process to assess advanced cyber capabilities of AI models and set the threshold at which a model is designated a "covered frontier model"; determination by NSA Director.
(b) design a VOLUNTARY framework where developers can: (i) ask the government whether a model qualifies as covered; (ii) give the government access to covered frontier models for up to 30 days before releasing to other trusted partners; (iii) collaborate on selecting trusted partners for early access.
(c) Nothing authorizes a mandatory licensing, preclearance, or permitting requirement for AI model development/release.

Sec. 4 Protection Against Criminal Actors. AG to prioritize enforcement of computer-fraud / wire-fraud statutes against AI-enabled illegal access, including "employing AI agents to unlawfully access data."

Sec. 5 General provisions (no new enforceable rights; subject to appropriations). Signed Donald J. Trump, June 2, 2026. (Related: Fact Sheet same day; NSPM-11 Jun 5; NSPM-12 Jun 12.)
